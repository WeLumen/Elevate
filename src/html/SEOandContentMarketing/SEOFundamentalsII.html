Lesson 2: SEO Fundamentals II

Keyword Research

Keyword research is one of the first and most important tasks in any SEO strategy. It involves identifying the words and phrases that potential users use to search for products, services, or information related to your business. This process helps to determine which terms to focus on to attract relevant traffic to your website.

The process includes the following steps:

Start by researching terms that your target audience is likely to search for. You can use tools like SEMrush or Sistrix (more tools are listed at the end of this section) to find relevant keywords, see their search volume, and understand the competition level.

Not all keywords are the same. It’s crucial to understand the intent behind each search (informational, transactional, navigational, or commercial). This analysis will help you prioritise the keywords that are most relevant to your business objectives.

Once you have identified and analysed keywords, choose those that are most relevant, achievable, and that offer the highest return on investment.

>

After selecting and prioritising keywords, the next step is to assign them to pages on your website, whether existing pages or new landing pages created specifically for those keywords. The goal is to ensure that each main keyword and its variants are associated with a page that clearly and effectively addresses the user's search intent.

  1. Existing Pages: Review your current website pages and see if any already adequately cover the selected keywords. If so, you can optimise them by naturally adjusting the content and other SEO elements (as we will see later) to ensure that these pages are perfectly aligned with the target keywords.
  2. Creating New Landing Pages: If you don’t have pages that match some important keywords, you’ll need to create new landing pages specifically for them. These pages should be designed to directly address the search intent and offer relevant and valuable content for the user.

When assigning keywords to specific pages, make sure not to over-optimise or assign multiple pages for the same keyword, as this can lead to internal competition (keyword cannibalisation) within your own website. The goal is for each page to be clearly focused on a main keyword and its variations, so that the search engine can easily identify its relevance.
