Lesson 1: Search Engine Marketing Strategy II

Differences between SEO and SEM

Earlier, we discussed paid ads and organic results. Organic results (SEO) are those that appear "naturally" based on the relevance and quality of a web page in relation to the search made. Paid ads (SEM), on the other hand, are results promoted through advertising campaigns and are displayed at the top of the search results page.

So, how exactly do SEO and SEM work?


SEO (search engine optimization)

This involves optimising a website so that it appears in organic or natural search results. It includes practices such as keyword research, content optimisation, improving page load speed, link building, among others. SEO is a medium/long-term strategy that can generate consistent and sustained traffic at a relatively low cost, as it doesn’t require direct payment for clicks.

SEM (search engine marketing)

This involves using paid ads to appear in search results. Through platforms like Google Ads, businesses can bid on specific keywords to ensure their ads appear at the top of search results. SEM can be an effective short-term strategy for generating immediate traffic, although it involves a direct cost each time a user clicks on an ad (pay-per-click or PPC).


SEO and SEM are different but complementary strategies, both crucial for improving a website's visibility in search engines and achieving business objectives. For example, SEM can be useful for specific campaigns and fast-selling products, while SEO is essential for building a strong and sustained digital presence over time.

From now on, we will focus solely on the SEO strategy. However, it's important to understand both to maximise the potential of a Search Engine Marketing strategy, which falls under a broader Digital Marketing strategy.
