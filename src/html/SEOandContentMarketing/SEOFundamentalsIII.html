Lesson 3: SEO Fundamentals III

Lesson 3: SEO Fundamentals III

Google's algorithm is the system through which the search engine organises and ranks the billions of web pages in its index to deliver the most relevant results to users. Although Google doesn’t reveal all the details of its algorithm, it is known to consider over 500 ranking factors.

These factors fall into two major categories: On-Page and Off-Page.

These are aspects directly controlled by the website owner. They include content quality and relevance, keyword optimisation, page structure, proper use of HTML tags (such as H1, H2), site loading speed, user experience (UX), and mobile compatibility. Good On-Page SEO ensures that Google understands the page’s content and considers it relevant to user queries.

These refer to external aspects that influence a website’s ranking. The most important factor in this category is backlinks—links from other websites pointing to your site. The more quality backlinks (from relevant and authoritative websites) a page has, the higher its authority in Google's eyes. Mentions on social media, branding efforts, and digital PR activities also influence Off-Page factors.

Google regularly updates its algorithm to provide better results for users. Some of these changes are minor, but others, like “Core Updates,” can have a significant impact on how pages are ranked. Therefore, it's crucial for SEO professionals to stay up to date with algorithm changes and adjust their strategies accordingly.
