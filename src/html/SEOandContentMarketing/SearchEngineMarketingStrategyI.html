Lesson 1: Search Engine Marketing Strategy I

The Importance of Search

Search engines play a fundamental role in the *Customer Journey*, which is the path a user follows from becoming aware of a brand to becoming a loyal customer. This process includes several stages: awareness, consideration, preference, website visit, purchase, user experience, loyalty, and recommendation.

Google is the most popular search engine. Depending on the country, there are other widely used search engines like Baidu (China), Yandex (Russia), or Bing (included by default in Microsoft systems).

Search is a crucial part of users' digital behaviour. Currently, around 3.5 billion searches are conducted on Google each day, highlighting the significant impact of search engines on people's daily lives and businesses.

This search process takes place during the initial stages of the Customer Journey (awareness, consideration, or preference), helping users resolve doubts, discover new products, or gather information about a service.

That is why optimising a business's presence on search engines, whether through paid or free strategies (as we will later discuss), is essential for attracting, converting, and retaining customers. Online searches are a powerful tool for connecting users with the brands and products they are looking for.

Google's Search Results Page

Google's Search Results Page (SERP) is where users interact with search results. This page displays different types of results: organic results, paid ads, featured snippets, knowledge panels, images, videos, and more.

The appearance of visual elements can change over time. Results may appear differently depending on whether you are using a computer or a phone, the country you are in, the language of your search query, and many other factors.

Any result accompanied by terms like "sponsored," "ad," etc., will correspond to a paid advertisement. Everything else corresponds to an organic result.

Understanding how the SERP is organised allows businesses to better plan their Search Engine Marketing strategies. For example, if you have a recipe website, it is very important for your content to appear in video format. However, if your website is for a hotel, you will likely need to use paid ads to appear in the top positions.
