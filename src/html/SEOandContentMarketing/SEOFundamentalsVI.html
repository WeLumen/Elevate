Lesson 6: SEO Fundamentals VI

Algorithm Updates

Google’s algorithm is continuously updated to improve the relevance of search results and provide a better user experience. These updates can have a significant impact on a website's visibility and traffic, making it crucial for SEO professionals to stay informed about major updates and understand how they affect the performance of their pages.

Minor and Major (Core) Updates

Google implements hundreds of updates each year, but most are minor adjustments. However, some updates are more significant and can substantially alter rankings. Examples of major updates include Google Panda, which penalised sites with low-quality content; Google Penguin, which focused on low-quality links and spam; and Google BERT, which enhanced the understanding of natural language.

Adapting to Changes
It is important for websites to remain aligned with best SEO practices, as these updates are designed to favour relevant, useful, and high-quality content. Staying up to date with changes in the algorithm allows SEO professionals to adjust their strategies quickly, avoid penalties, and seize new opportunities to improve rankings.
Official Sources and Monitoring
It’s essential to remember that SEO is not static, and algorithm updates are a constant in the work of SEO professionals. Staying informed and acting accordingly is key to maintaining and improving visibility in search engines.

    • Summary

  • SEO begins with the crawling, indexing, and ranking of web pages. Optimising the content and structure of a site makes the job easier for search engines and improves rankings.
  • Keyword research is crucial to identify the most relevant terms that users search for. The selected keywords are assigned to existing pages or new landing pages to optimise their relevance.
  • Google considers over 500 factors to rank pages. On-Page factors (content, structure, speed) and Off-Page factors (backlinks, authority) are essential for SEO.
  • Content should be original, useful, and well-structured, with a natural use of keywords. Optimising titles, meta descriptions, and using multimedia enhances both user experience and SEO.
  • Loading speed and user experience are key factors in SEO. Optimising Core Web Vitals and ensuring proper mobile performance (Mobile-First Indexing) is essential to improve rankings and reduce bounce rates.
  • Google updates its algorithm regularly. Staying informed about these updates allows SEO professionals to adjust their strategies to maintain and improve visibility in search results.
