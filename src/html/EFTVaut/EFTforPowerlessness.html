Lesson 3: EFT for Powerlessness

If you’re reading this, it may be because something inside you has been feeling tender… perhaps raw, tired, or unsure of how to keep moving forward. Maybe you’ve been carrying a weight you don’t quite know how to put down — a quiet ache of powerlessness, like you’ve lost your voice, your footing, or your sense of self.

Please know: there is nothing wrong with you. Powerlessness is not a flaw. It is a signal — a sacred whisper from within that longs to be met with love.

As a holistic therapist and EFT practitioner, I want to remind you that these feelings aren’t your identity. They’re echoes — often rooted in unmet needs, unresolved pain, or inherited patterns that once helped you survive but no longer serve your soul’s truth.

And the good news? You can transmute this. Gently. Tenderly. At your own pace.

Together, we’ll journey inward — not to fix you, but to remember you. To return to your center. To reclaim the inner power that has always lived inside you — perhaps hidden, perhaps quiet, but never lost.

Grounding Into the Body

Let’s begin by coming home to the body — your first and truest sanctuary.

Place one hand over your heart, and the other over your belly if it feels good to do so. Close your eyes, or soften your gaze.

Take a slow, nourishing breath in through your nose…
Hold it for 3… 2… 1…
Exhale gently through the mouth.

Again, inhale peace…
Hold for 3… 2… 1…
Exhale tension, doubt, fear.

One more breath. This time, imagine breathing in yourself — your truth, your light.
Hold for 3… 2… 1…
Exhale anything that no longer belongs.

You are safe here. Your body is listening. And your healing begins now.

EFT Tapping Practice: Releasing Powerlessness

Karate Chop Point (Side of the Hand)

Repeat gently after me — aloud or silently in your heart:

Even though I feel powerless right now… I deeply and completely love and accept myself.
Even though I feel like I’ve lost control, like I don’t know the way… I hold myself with love and grace.
Even though I sometimes feel like I don’t matter… I honor that pain — and I honor the part of me that longs to rise.

Tapping Through the Points

Eyebrow Point: I feel so small right now… like nothing I do will make a difference.

Side of the Eye: I’ve tried to be strong… but I feel like I’m carrying too much.

Under the Eye: I feel invisible sometimes… like no one really sees me.

Under the Nose: I’m afraid I’ll keep failing… that I’ll never fully become who I know I’m meant to be.

Chin Point: There’s a deep fear… that I’m not enough.

Collarbone Point: Maybe I internalized someone else’s voice… A parent, a teacher, a partner — someone who made me question my worth.

Under the Arm: I’ve been holding this for so long… These stories… these wounds… these burdens that were never mine to carry.

Top of the Head: I am so ready to release this weight.

Tapping Into Healing & Reclamation

Eyebrow Point: I am not broken. I am becoming.

Side of the Eye: I am not powerless — I am healing, layer by layer.

Under the Eye: I am allowed to take up space. I am allowed to change.

Under the Nose: There is strength in my softness.

Chin Point: My power is not loud or forceful. It is steady. Gentle. Sacred.

Collarbone Point: I am remembering who I truly am.

Under the Arm: I choose to believe in myself — even if it’s just a whisper right now.

Top of the Head: I am reclaiming my voice, my worth, my power.

Empowering Integration: Affirmations for the Soul

I was never meant to stay small.
I am here to grow — to rise — to bloom.
I choose to step forward, even when I feel afraid.
I choose to love myself through every phase of becoming.
I trust my intuition.
I trust my timing.
I trust my strength — even when it trembles.
I am no longer available for stories that keep me stuck.
I am a powerful woman with a sacred, sovereign light.
And I will not dim it for anyone.
I am becoming all of who I came here to be.

Closing the Practice

Place your hands over your heart again.

Breathe in deeply…
Hold…
Exhale gently.

Ask yourself — with kindness, not judgment:
“If my feeling of powerlessness was a 10 before, where is it now?”

Let whatever arises be enough.

Maybe it’s a 7. Maybe it’s a 3. Maybe there are tears — or maybe just stillness. All of it is welcome. All of it is healing.

Let this be a space you return to whenever the world feels too heavy.

You are not behind.
You are not weak.
You are awakening.
And even when it feels messy — that, too, is part of the process.

I see your courage. I honor your healing. And I trust in your power, even when you can’t quite feel it yet.

You are not alone, dear one. You are a radiant, powerful being — and you are rising.

With love, always.
— Your Inner Guide & Gentle Mirror
