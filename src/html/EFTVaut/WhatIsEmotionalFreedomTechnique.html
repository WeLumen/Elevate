Lesson 1: What Is Emotional Freedom Technique

A Sacred Bridge Between Emotion and Energy

Emotional Freedom Technique — or EFT, often lovingly referred to as “tapping” — is a powerful yet gentle modality that blends modern psychology with ancient Eastern wisdom. At its heart, it’s an energy healing practice that uses gentle tapping on specific acupressure points to release emotional stagnation, shift limiting beliefs, and restore the body’s natural energetic flow.

Think of it as acupuncture for your emotions — but without the needles. Instead, we use our fingertips to rhythmically tap on the body’s meridian points, calming the nervous system, quieting the survival brain, and inviting in a new emotional state.

And here’s the truly magical part: your body already knows how to heal. EFT simply opens the space for that healing to rise to the surface.

Why Does EFT Work?

From a clinical perspective, EFT works by gently disrupting old emotional patterns stored in the amygdala — the part of our brain responsible for fear, anxiety, and stress responses. By naming what we’re feeling and simultaneously tapping on meridian points, we send a soothing signal to the brain that says: “It’s safe to feel this. I’m safe now.”

Energetically, EFT works by moving stuck chi — life force energy — that often gets trapped in the body due to trauma, stress, or unprocessed emotions. These blocks can show up as physical tension, emotional overwhelm, procrastination, or looping thoughts. With EFT, we melt those blocks gently and compassionately.

Spiritually, EFT is a form of self-love. It’s a practice of presence — an embodied prayer. It allows us to come home to ourselves, one tender tap at a time.

The Healing Philosophy Behind EFT

As a holistic guide, I believe EFT is not about “fixing” you. You are not broken. Your behaviors, your reactions, even your emotional pain — they’re all messengers. They’re protective patterns your system developed to keep you safe. EFT doesn’t fight these patterns; it honors them. It listens to their story. And when they feel truly seen, they can soften and evolve.

This practice is deeply trauma-sensitive. We don’t force breakthroughs. We hold space. We move gently. We tap with reverence.

What EFT Can Support

  • Emotional overwhelm or anxiety
  • Stress and burnout
  • Limiting beliefs or “inner critic” voices
  • Childhood wounds and inner child healing
  • Fear of being seen or fear of failure
  • Relationship patterns
  • Procrastination and perfectionism
  • Nervous system dysregulation
  • And so much more...

Many of my clients come to EFT when they’ve tried mindset work but still feel stuck in the same old patterns. Because the truth is — transformation isn’t just mental. It’s somatic. The body holds the memory. And the body must be part of the healing.

The Tapping Points: A Sacred Map

Let’s walk through each point as though we’re mapping a journey — not just across your skin, but through your own internal landscape.

  • Karate Chop Point (Side of the Hand): This is our grounding point. It’s where we begin — acknowledging what’s true, even if it’s uncomfortable. “Even though I feel ________, I deeply and completely love and accept myself.”
  • Eyebrow (Beginning of the Brow, Near the Bridge of the Nose): This is where we begin to gently touch the emotion. We name it, without judgment. “This heavy feeling... it’s here right now.”
  • Side of the Eye (On the Temple): Tapping here softens tension and brings safety to the body. “I feel overwhelmed. And it’s okay to feel this.”
  • Under the Eye (Top of the Cheekbone): A powerful point for fear, worry, and old grief. “I’ve carried this for so long.”
  • Under the Nose (Between Nose and Upper Lip): This point touches on self-worth and identity. “Part of me feels I’m not enough…”
  • Chin Point (Midway Between Bottom Lip and Chin): A place for releasing shame or feeling stuck. “It’s hard to let this go… and I’m trying.”
  • Collarbone (Just Under the Clavicle Bones): A favorite — this point taps into the heart and lungs. It brings deep emotional release and grounding. “I give myself permission to exhale this.”
  • Under the Arm (About 4 Inches Below the Armpit): This point often holds stress, especially around relationships or fear of change. “It’s safe for me to release this.”
  • Crown of the Head (Top of the Scalp): The integrator. The point of reconnection. “I am choosing peace. I am safe in my body.”

A Note on Affirmations in EFT

You might notice that we begin with the “negative” — this isn’t to reinforce it, but to honor it. Healing doesn’t come from bypassing or sugarcoating. It comes from being present with what is. Once the emotional charge is met and soothed, we gently pivot toward possibility.

We might begin with:

“Even though I feel stuck and afraid, I accept myself with kindness.”

And move toward:

“I am open to healing. I am allowed to grow beyond this.”

Final Thoughts from My Heart to Yours

EFT is more than a technique — it’s a sacred practice of returning to wholeness. Every tap is a message to your body: You are safe now. You are loved. You are healing.

So whether you’re tapping through tears or simply breathing into presence… you are doing the work. You are unwinding generational patterns. You are creating space for the woman you’re becoming.

And that, beautiful soul, is everything.
