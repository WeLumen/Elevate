Lesson 1: Introduction to Somatics

So, what exactly is somatics?

At its core, the word soma means body. Somatics is the study and practice of engaging with the body— not just through movement, but through the internal, felt experience. It’s about noticing physical sensations, exploring how emotions show up in the body, and learning to understand the body’s own language.

Rather than always focusing on the mind, somatics invites us to drop into the body’s perspective. This internal awareness allows us to bypass constant mental analysis and access a deeper layer of knowing. Sensations, feelings, and even imagery can offer valuable insight into what’s truly going on within us.

By tuning into these physical sensations, we begin to recognize how emotions and beliefs manifest in the body. This awareness can bring clarity, especially when it comes to uncovering unconscious patterns. In this way, somatics becomes a powerful tool—not only for healing, but for aligned manifestation and deeper self-awareness.

The body holds wisdom. It’s constantly offering signals and cues. When we become more present and curious about what we feel, we can access that wisdom and begin to create space for transformation.

The Nervous System Connection

A key component in somatic work is the nervous system. The nervous system is an integrated network that runs from the brain throughout the body, signaling when we’re in balance—and when we’re not. These signals show up as tension, fatigue, restlessness, or other sensations, and they offer an invitation to pause and listen. Becoming aware of these responses helps us regulate our state and shift toward grounded, embodied presence.

A Gentle Beginning

As you begin this journey, approach somatics with curiosity rather than pressure to "get it right." Let the body guide you. Try this simple shift: next time you feel overwhelmed, say “hmm…” This small sound softens internal tension, shifts neural pathways, and invites spaciousness. Instead of reacting, ask: “Hmm, what’s happening in my body right now?” That question alone can open the door to deeper insight.

In the next video, simple somatic practices will be introduced to begin building your personal connection with the body. Remember: somatic work is a conversation. It’s about how you listen, how you respond, and how you honor the signals your body sends. Even ordinary moments—like noticing how you feel while drinking water or taking a shower—can become opportunities to deepen your awareness.

Stay curious, stay open, and allow your body to guide the way.
