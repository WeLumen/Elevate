Lesson 1: Feeling your Feelings Meditation

I’m so glad you’re here. I’m Rita May, and I’m here as your loving guide through this tender journey.

This meditation is a soft landing place for the moments when emotions feel heavy… and you find yourself wanting to reach for food—not out of hunger, but as a way to soothe, to quiet, or to escape the storm inside.

If you’re feeling…

  • Anxious
  • Sad
  • Lonely
  • Bored
  • Angry
  • Frustrated
  • Afraid
  • Ashamed
  • Embarrassed
  • Exhausted
  • Jealous
  • Heartbroken
  • Hopeless
  • Enraged
  • Impatient

…or anything else that feels overwhelming or hard to hold—this is your sanctuary. A place where you don’t have to run, numb, or push away what you’re feeling. A place where your emotions are met with compassion.

This practice is an invitation to be with yourself lovingly, just as you are. It will support you in gently processing your emotions and coming back to a more peaceful, centered state. You’ll hear affirmations that speak directly to your heart and subconscious, helping shift old patterns into new ways of relating to yourself—with grace and care.

So, find a quiet, cozy spot. Let your body settle.
Place a hand on your heart, or wherever feels soothing.
And when you’re ready, take a deep, nourishing breath in… hold it gently… and now exhale slowly through your mouth.
Let’s begin.

I know, deep down, that when I try to ignore, push down, or numb my feelings with food, the relief is only temporary—and the pain stays underneath. And eventually, I still have to face both the emotion and the discomfort of overeating.

Using food to cover up what I feel is like placing a bandage on a wound that needs care and attention. I may not want to look at it… but I no longer want to live in fear of my own emotions.

I am safe to feel.
I am now willing to gently turn toward my feelings instead of running from them.

I allow whatever emotion is present to simply… be here.
I soften into the knowing that it cannot harm me.
It is only a feeling. And feelings are meant to flow through.

So I pause.
I ask myself: What am I feeling right now?
I name it.
I welcome it.

Hello, dear anxiety… sadness… loneliness… whatever is here.
I see you.
You are part of my human experience.
I breathe and allow space for this emotion.
It may feel uncomfortable, and that’s okay. I may cry. I may scream into a pillow. I may journal it out. However it wants to move through me, I trust the process.

I remind myself:
It’s okay not to feel okay today.
It’s okay not to feel joyful or beautiful or successful right now.
It’s okay to be right here, in the rawness of this moment.
Because all feelings come and go.
This one will, too.

I don’t need to fix this feeling or force it to leave.
I simply let it move through me, like a wave that rises and then falls.

I take a deep breath in… and out.
And I gently tune into my body.
Where am I feeling this emotion right now?
My chest? My throat? My stomach? My shoulders?

I just notice. I breathe.
And with each breath, I feel the emotion begin to soften, shift, release.

I breathe in peace…
And I breathe out the heaviness.
I breathe in calm…
And I breathe out what no longer serves me.
I breathe in love…
And I breathe out fear.

There is only me and my breath.
In… and out.
In… and out.

And now, with love and intention, I imagine myself in a peaceful place—a sanctuary in nature, where my soul feels held. I see myself holding a big, red balloon by a string.

I gather all of my heavy emotions and gently place them inside the balloon. Every ounce of sadness, stress, frustration, fear… I let it all go into the balloon.

And then, I slowly release the string…
I watch the balloon float higher and higher… carrying all of that weight away from me… until it’s just a tiny speck in the sky… and then, it’s gone.

I whisper softly…
“Goodbye, dear anxiety… thank you for the message. You can rest now.”

And now, I allow a wave of peace to gently flow over me.
Starting at the top of my head, moving down through my face, my neck, my arms, my chest… down through my belly, my hips, my legs, and feet.

My whole body feels relaxed.
Held.
Safe.
Present.
Alive.

I am here. I am okay. I am enough.

Take a few more deep, loving breaths.
And when you're ready… slowly bring your awareness back to the room.
Carry this softness with you as you move through the rest of your day.

With all my heart,
Thank you for showing up for yourself today.
You are so worthy of love, exactly as you are.

Nice work! This Lesson is complete.
