Lesson 5: Stop Cravings Meditation

It's perfectly okay to experience cravings—there's no need for worry or guilt. Let’s take a moment together to explore what this craving might be trying to communicate.

Exploring Your Craving

What specific food are you craving right now? How much of it would feel satisfying to you? Is the feeling coming from your tummy or your mind? It’s okay if you're unsure; let’s take a moment to tune in more deeply.

Why do you feel like eating right now?

  • Do you feel a grumble or emptiness in your stomach?
  • Perhaps you’re feeling lightheaded, shaky, or a bit grumpy?
  • Maybe you even have a headache?
  • Has it been a few hours since your last meal?
  • Does a certain food sound appealing right now?

If your body is signaling it’s time for nourishment, go ahead and enjoy something that feels nurturing and fulfilling. Imagine eating the portion that will truly satisfy you. How much do you need to feel content? Where would you like to enjoy this meal, and who would you want to share it with? Consider what would feel most loving and nourishing to you right now.

As you eat, remember to do so slowly and mindfully. Notice the sensations in your body as you eat. Feel your stomach filling and take deep breaths, connecting with your diaphragm to sense when you've had enough. Allow yourself to experience the satisfaction of being nourished, without guilt or shame. Even if the choice isn't the healthiest or you eat more than you intended, simply embrace the feeling of being satiated.

When you feel satisfied, allow your body to relax and begin the beautiful process of digestion. There’s no need for stress or anxiety—just let it go.

When Cravings Aren’t Physical

If your craving isn’t rooted in physical need but arises from something deeper—perhaps a desire for a specific food even after you’ve eaten—let’s explore that. Was there something that triggered this craving? A thought, a sight, a smell, or maybe seeing someone else eat? Sometimes, food becomes a way to seek comfort or sensory stimulation.

Is there a habit tied to this time of day that leads you to crave something, even when you’re not hungry?

  • Perhaps you’re procrastinating, using food as a way to avoid something difficult, or maybe you’re simply between tasks and feel like having something to do.
  • Are you tired and seeking comfort, or maybe trying to boost your energy with food?
  • Could it be that you feel like you deserve a treat?

Take a moment to reflect on what this craving might really be about. Could it be a longing for something beyond food? Perhaps it’s time to sit with your feelings and truly understand what’s going on inside.

Identifying the Thought Behind the Craving

When a craving arises, ask yourself: What’s behind this feeling? What thought triggered it? Can you identify the thought instead of acting on it immediately? Give yourself permission to pause and listen.

What did that thought say to you? How did it make you feel? Consider the environment you're in—could it be influencing you? Perhaps a person or situation is triggering you. Are there emotions or unmet needs calling for your attention?

It’s okay to feel this way. Cravings don’t last forever. Take a deep breath and explore the stories or thoughts that might be intensifying the situation. Are you dramatizing the moment, or is your reality actually better than your mind is telling you? When we start to catastrophize, it often signals that we’re seeking an escape.

What story are you telling yourself that might not be true? On a scale of 1 to 10, how intense is this craving? What and how much food would truly satisfy you right now? Sometimes, a small portion is all it takes. Remember, you have choices.

Visualize yourself eating the portion you need to feel content. How much food would that take? Where would you enjoy it, and who would you like to share it with? Take time to consider what truly supports your well-being in this moment.

You may find that waiting for a better time to enjoy your food helps reduce the urgency around it. Planning your meal and letting go of scarcity can make the experience feel less tempting.

Reflecting on the Consequences

Now, pause and reflect on how you’ll feel after indulging this craving. Close your eyes and imagine the experience: how will you feel immediately afterward? How about 5 minutes later, or a few hours down the road? What about tomorrow or next week, if you give in to every craving?

Consider both the positive and negative consequences, but approach them with curiosity—not guilt. You are exploring with self-respect and self-compassion. What do you want to feel in your body in the next few minutes, hours, or even days?

How do you want your energy to be? How do you want to feel—truly feel—after this moment?

Mindfully Indulging Your Craving

If your craving is strong, it’s okay to indulge in a reasonable portion. Just remember to do so mindfully, paying attention to the feelings of satisfaction as you eat. Feel your stomach as it fills and listen to your body when it tells you that you’ve had enough.

If you find yourself wanting more, remind yourself that you can always enjoy this food again tomorrow. There’s no rush—there’s always time to nourish yourself.

When the Craving Isn’t as Strong

If your craving isn’t as strong, try to find comfort in something other than food. Practice feeling good in the moment without eating. Ask yourself:

  • What do I need right now, besides food?
  • What will food give me in this moment?
  • What are my true needs and desires right now?
  • Am I seeking comfort, peace, rest, or perhaps a conversation?
  • Do I need a distraction, some pleasure, or a boost of confidence?
  • Maybe what I need is reassurance that I am doing great.

You are doing great. You are enough. You are more than enough. So, what do you truly need right now? How can you show yourself love, comfort, and compassion?

True Comfort and Self-Compassion

Remember, comfort isn’t about numbing your feelings or using food to cover up what’s really going on. True comfort comes from being present with yourself, understanding what’s behind the craving, and honoring your needs.

If you're unsure of what you need, just sit with the question and give yourself the space to listen. You're doing the beautiful work of tuning into yourself. You are so much more than this craving, and you are doing beautifully.
