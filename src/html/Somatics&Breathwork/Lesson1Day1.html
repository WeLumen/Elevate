Lesson 1: Day 1

This journey is for anyone seeking hope and healing from Interstitial Cystitis (IC) and painful bladder syndrome. Over the next six days, you’ll be introduced to essential steps that can help support your path to relief and restoration.

This video series includes six segments, each offering a key takeaway—something you can begin applying right away. You can watch them at your own pace, one per day or all at once. This is your journey, and you have the power to move through it in the way that feels right for you.

Let’s begin with Step 1: Commitment.

Healing starts with a decision—a deep, personal commitment to move toward wellness, even when the path feels uncertain. This doesn’t mean everything will be easy. There may be moments of doubt, but choosing to commit is the foundation for progress.

Those who move toward healing most effectively often carry a sense of belief in their body’s ability to recover. They treat healing not as a distant goal, but as something possible now. This belief isn’t about perfection—it’s about persistence and self-trust.

Start today by making that commitment to yourself.

You don’t have to believe everything right away. Just begin by deciding that you're ready to explore healing. That first step, no matter how small, sets everything else in motion.

A simple tool to support this process is journaling.

Writing down your thoughts, doubts, hopes, and goals can be a powerful way to reconnect with yourself. If you’re unsure where to begin, just reflect on your current feelings, your fears, and your desire for healing. Use your journal as a safe space to get honest and clear with yourself.

And most importantly, make time for yourself.

Living with IC can often lead to putting your own needs last. But healing starts when you prioritize your wellbeing. Set aside just a little time each day—15 to 30 minutes—just for you. Use this time to rest, reflect, and care for your body and mind.

As you move through this series, the intention is to help you build a sense of trust in your own healing process. Progress may be gradual, but each step matters.

Today’s takeaway: Make the commitment.

Write it down. Say it aloud. Declare it to yourself. You are taking your power back, one moment at a time.

You are worthy. You are capable. And this journey is yours to shape.
