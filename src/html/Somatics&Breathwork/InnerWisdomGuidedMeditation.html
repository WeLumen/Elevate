Lesson 4: Inner Wisdom Guided Meditation

Each day, our thoughts and feelings flow and shift, just as the world around us moves at such a fast pace.

It can be a challenge to keep up with the constant ebb and flow of our minds, let alone to truly understand and make sense of them.

But as you begin to develop your mindfulness practice, something beautiful begins to unfold. Like a calm pond where the water slowly clears, your mind begins to settle, becoming more transparent and open. This creates space for you to connect with your inner wisdom in ways that feel gentle and grounded.

With time, you’ll notice your ability to observe your thoughts and feelings with more clarity. This observation isn’t about judgment, but about understanding—especially in how you relate to yourself with kindness and wisdom.

As you continue to cultivate this practice, you’ll find that you are able to look at your thoughts, behaviors, habits, and life with a fresh, more compassionate perspective.

Sasha Loring, a psychotherapist and meditation teacher, has developed a model that describes the mind in three layers.

If you’re new to meditation, don’t worry if you haven’t yet touched all of these layers. Remember the metaphor of muddy water—it takes time for clarity to emerge.

The Three Layers of the Mind

The first layer, which is the most accessible, is often called the “monkey mind” or the chattering mind. This is where the constant stream of thoughts, judgments, and distractions take place, and it can feel quite difficult to quiet.

Beneath that is the second layer—the reactive mind. This layer is where thoughts, beliefs, emotions, and sensations that influence our behavior reside. These often stem from deep-seated beliefs and can sometimes create a fog that clouds our view.

And then, at the deepest level, there is the wisest part of you—the deepest layer of your mind, where your inner wisdom resides.

Your wisest mind, your inner guidance, can only emerge when the top two layers are seen and understood for what they are, and when the noise quiets down.

Through consistent meditation, you can cultivate the peace needed to quiet the busy mind and create space for this deep wisdom to shine through.

Your Inner Compass

Think of your wisest mind as your Inner Compass—your internal guide that always has your best interests at heart.

When you follow this inner guidance, you naturally align your actions with what truly matters to you, living in harmony with your values. The Inner Compass doesn’t come from a place of “shoulds” or external pressures—it comes from a space of authenticity and inner motivation.

In the process, you will learn to recognize your Inner Compass from the other voices in your mind—like the Inner Critic, or the part of you that’s driven by immediate wants and desires. These voices often tell you what you “should” do, but they’re not always aligned with what you truly care about.

Let’s take a moment now to connect with your Inner Compass. It’s always there, guiding you, loving you, and leading you toward a more peaceful and fulfilled life.
