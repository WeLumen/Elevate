Lesson 2: Somatic Exercices

Now that we’ve laid the foundation of what somatics truly means—coming home to the body and listening to its language—let’s gently begin exploring some simple, powerful practices you can integrate into your daily life.

These exercises are here to support you in reconnecting with your physical self—to feel how your body moves, how it communicates, and how it stores wisdom, emotion, and sensation. You don't need to "get it right" or perform these perfectly. Let that idea go. There is no right or wrong way here. This is about presence, not performance.

So often, we’ve been taught to “do” instead of feel, to “fix” rather than listen. But true healing happens when we meet ourselves exactly as we are—with curiosity, softness, and care.

✧ The Orientation Practice

Let’s begin with a grounding technique that helps bring you into the present moment: the Orientation Practice. This simple exercise can help reset the nervous system and create a sense of safety in the body. You can do it anywhere, anytime.

Take a breath in…and as you exhale, begin here:

  • Five: Name five things you can see in your environment. Let your gaze move gently. No rush.
  • Four: Name four things you can touch. Maybe it's the texture of your clothing, the ground beneath your feet, or the surface of a nearby object.
  • Three: Name three things you can hear. Let yourself listen deeply—what’s close, what’s far?
  • Two: Name two things you can taste. Perhaps a sip of water, or just the natural flavor of your mouth.
  • One: Name one thing you can smell. Breathe it in fully, whatever it may be.

This practice helps reorient the body in time and space. It reminds your nervous system: I am safe. I am here. I belong.

And if emotions come up while doing this—grief, fear, resistance—that’s okay too. Let them move through. It’s not about suppressing what you feel, but gently holding space for it.

✧ The Breath Check-In

Here’s something surprisingly powerful: simply asking yourself—“Am I breathing?”

So often, we unconsciously hold our breath, especially in moments of stress or focus. The next time you notice tension creeping in, pause. Gently inhale... then exhale slowly, letting your shoulders drop as you do.

Try setting a reminder throughout the day. A soft chime or vibration to bring your awareness back to your breath. With each breath, you’re sending a signal of safety and presence to your entire system. You’re reminding your body: I’m with you. I’m listening.

✧ Mindful Tooth Brushing

Yes, even brushing your teeth can become a sacred somatic ritual.

Instead of rushing through it, use it as a moment of mindfulness. Notice the scent of the toothpaste, the texture in your hands, the sound of the bristles, the sensation on your gums. Bring all of your attention into this moment.

This small act becomes a portal to presence. It’s about welcoming your body back into the conversation. Not just brushing teeth, but experiencing it fully.

This isn’t about doing more—it’s about doing with awareness. It’s a gentle remembering that your body is always with you, always speaking. You just need to pause and listen.

In the next part of this journey, we’ll explore how your somatic awareness can flow into your business, creativity, and service to the world. Because when your body feels heard, supported, and honored, you show up differently—more aligned, more clear, and more whole.

For now, simply play. Explore. Let these practices be invitations rather than obligations. Let them meet you exactly where you are. Your body is not a problem to solve—it’s your wisest guide.

Thank you for being here with yourself today.
