Lesson 1: Understand: The Psychology of Self-Criticism Befriending Your Inner Protector

She’s been trying to keep you safe from judgment, rejection, failure, and the very real pain that can come when we show up fully in the world. The voice that says “You’re not enough” or “Don’t make a mistake”? It was born out of fear and a need to protect you from hurt.

You’ll learn how, over time, this protective voice became wired into your brain and body through constant repetition—becoming so familiar it feels like you. The most important lesson this week? You don’t have to fight or silence this voice. Instead, you’ll practice meeting her with empathy and compassion.

Reflective Journaling Prompts: Befriending Your Inner Critic

  • Where did I first hear the voice of my Inner Critic?
  • Can I remember a moment from childhood or early life when this voice felt loudest? What was happening? Who was around?
  • What is my Inner Critic really trying to protect me from?
  • What fears or vulnerabilities might be behind its harsh words? How might it be trying to keep me safe, even if it feels painful or unfair?
  • In what situations does my Inner Critic show up strongest?
  • How do I usually respond to this voice?
  • What would it look like to respond to my Inner Critic with kindness instead of resistance?
  • What positive intention might my Inner Critic have, if it could speak openly?

Meditation: Meeting Your Inner Critic with Compassion

Find a quiet space. Sit comfortably with your eyes closed or softly gazing down. Take three deep, grounding breaths.

Begin by inviting your Inner Critic to come forward in your mind’s eye. Picture her as she usually shows up—a voice, a feeling, an image. Notice any emotions or sensations that arise as you bring her into awareness. Where do you feel her in your body? What does she say? Listen without judgment.

Now, soften your energy. Imagine wrapping her in a warm, gentle light of kindness and curiosity. Tell her softly, “I see you. I know you’re trying to help me, even if your way feels hard.”

Ask her gently, “What do you need from me? How can I support you?” Listen patiently. Thank her for her protection and bravery. Let her know you’re ready to take the lead now—with courage, love, and wisdom.

Take a few more deep breaths, feeling this new connection settle in. When you’re ready, slowly open your eyes.
