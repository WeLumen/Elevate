Lesson 2: Guided Hypnotherapy Audio Script: Meet Your Empowered Self

Begin by finding a quiet, comfortable place to sit or lie down. Take a deep breath and close your eyes.

Coach (calm, soothing voice):

Welcome.

Today, you’re stepping into a space of deep relaxation and profound connection where your future empowered self awaits to meet you.

Take a slow, deep breath in... and let it go with ease.

Feel your body soften, your mind slow, and your heart open.

With each breath, you sink deeper into calm... deeper into safety... deeper into yourself.

Now, imagine in front of you a soft, glowing light—warm and inviting, like a sunrise.

This light is a portal—a doorway to your future self. The woman you are becoming, radiant with strength, clarity, and purpose.

Step through this light and see her waiting for you.

Notice how she stands—tall, grounded, and relaxed.

Feel the energy she carries a magnetic, quiet power that draws you in.

She smiles warmly, eyes full of love and knowing.

This woman has walked the path you are on, faced the fears, and stepped boldly into her truth.

Now, hear her voice as she speaks to you words meant just for your soul:

“You are whole. You are worthy. All the power you seek has always lived inside you. Trust your journey. Trust your heart. I am here with you—always.”

Feel her energy flow into you now, like a wave of light filling every cell.

Feel your confidence deepen, your clarity sharpen, your courage ignite.

Take a moment to sit with this feeling your empowered self is alive within you, ready to guide and support you.

When you’re ready, gently bring your awareness back to your breath.

Feel your body in this space.

Wiggle your fingers and toes.

And when it feels right, open your eyes.

Carry this knowing with you your empowered self is always with you, a source of light and strength.

Thank you for showing up to this moment of transformation.

Practice: Your Empowered Woman Declaration

This is where it all comes together.

You’ve done the work. You’ve looked within, peeled back the layers, met your truth, and reclaimed so many parts of yourself.

Now—it’s time to own who you’re becoming. With your words. With your presence. With your voice.

This isn’t just about writing something down. It’s about declaring—out loud—the woman you now choose to be.

What is a Declaration?

It’s not a script. It’s not a slogan. It’s your truth, spoken with heart.

This is your "I’m no longer hiding" moment.

Your “this is who I am now” moment.

It’s a living statement that reflects your values, your boundaries, your voice, and your vision.

It’s who you are when you stop shrinking and start showing up fully.

Step One: Reflect + Write

Take a breath. Put your hand on your heart. Feel into these questions—not from your head, but from your body.

Let your truth rise up from your chest, your belly, your bones.

Ask yourself:

  • Who am I when I’m not trying to prove or perform?
  • What do I now stand for without apology?
  • What have I outgrown?
  • What am I ready to fully claim?
  • What kind of woman do I want to be... when no one’s watching?

Now start to shape your answers into a declaration something you can read, say, feel. It might sound like:

“I am a woman who trusts herself even when it’s hard. I no longer wait to be chosen. I choose me. I speak with clarity. I lead with love. I take up space without guilt. My power is not a performance—it’s who I am when I’m rooted and real.”

There’s no right or wrong here. Just your truth.

Step Two: Speak It

Once you’ve written your declaration, take it to the mirror. Look yourself in the eyes.

Yes it might feel awkward. That’s okay. This isn’t about sounding perfect. It’s about being real.

Read it slowly. Out loud. Let the words land in your body. Let yourself feel what you’re saying.

Speak like you believe it… even if your voice shakes a little. (Especially if your voice shakes a little.)

You’re reprogramming something deep here.

Step Three: Keep It Close

This declaration isn’t a one-time thing.

Stick it on your mirror. Make it the lock screen on your phone. Read it every morning before your day begins. Speak it when you feel doubt creeping back in.

Let this be your anchor the thing that reminds you of who you are when the world tries to make you forget.

A Loving Reminder

You don’t need to become someone else. You’re not creating a new identity. You’re remembering the woman you’ve always been—before the world told you to be smaller, quieter, nicer.

Now… you get to decide.

Who are you when you trust yourself? Who are you when you stop waiting? Who are you when you finally, finally speak your truth?

Write that woman into existence. Then go live her. One breath, one word, one bold moment at a time.

Nice work! This Lesson is complete.
