Lesson 1: Why Networking Matters Especially for Women

“The way you communicate is not just about what you say. It’s a reflection of how you see yourself.”

Welcome to Your First Step: Confidence Is Your Foundation

Before diving into techniques or scripts, we start with you. Your voice, presence, and energy create deeper connections than any clever talking points ever could.

Before you walk into your next networking event or strike up a conversation at a coffee shop, ask yourself:

“Do I trust the sound of my own voice?”

This module will help you:

  • Identify the internal and external layers of your communication style.
  • Reclaim your power in conversations.
  • Build a foundation of self-trust that radiates through every word you speak.

Key Lessons Breakdown

1. Why Networking Matters — Especially for Women

Networking is not a nicety — it’s a necessity.

Women have often been socialized to wait to be noticed, to prioritize relationship-building over self-promotion, and to avoid being perceived as “too forward.” The result? Many women feel awkward or inauthentic in networking spaces that reward loud or traditionally male-coded communication styles.

The truth is:

  • Networking is not about being extroverted.
  • It’s not about handing out business cards or collecting contacts.
  • It’s about building relationships that align with your values, goals, and vision.

In this section, you’ll explore:

  • Why women tend to underestimate their social capital.
  • How your network influences career advancement, visibility, and opportunities.
  • The difference between transactional and transformational networking.
  • How to leverage your natural strengths — empathy, active listening, and intuition — as networking superpowers.
“Networking doesn’t mean changing who you are — it means being more of who you are, on purpose.”

Scripted Scenario: Transformational vs. Transactional Networking

Scenario A: Transactional (What NOT to Do)

Setting: A networking event.

You: “Hi, I’m Jenna. I work in digital marketing. Do you know if your company’s hiring?”

Them: “Uh… not sure.”

You: “Okay. Here’s my card.” walks away

Feels: rushed, impersonal, and forgettable.

Scenario B: Transformational (What TO Do)

You: “Hi, I’m Jenna. I work in digital marketing, and I’m passionate about helping small brands grow online. I’ve been exploring roles where I can collaborate with teams that value creative autonomy. What kind of projects light you up lately?”

Them: “Oh, I actually love brand strategy — especially on the visual side.”

You: “That’s awesome! Have you worked with any purpose-driven brands recently?”

Feels: curious, authentic, and builds connection beyond surface level.

Role Play Prompt

  • Partner A: Plays someone who finds networking awkward.
  • Partner B: Practices leading with curiosity, not credentials.
  • Goal: Shift from “Let me pitch myself” to “Let me connect human to human.”
