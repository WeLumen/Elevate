Lesson 1: Discover Your Communication Baseline

“Your voice doesn’t have to be loud to be powerful. It just has to be yours.”

Before a single word is spoken, the room is already reading you. Your energy walks in first. And here’s the truth most of us were never taught: You have the power to shape the story your body and voice are telling.

This section is your invitation to reclaim that power with presence, intention, and grounded confidence.

Discover Your Communication Baseline

Let’s start with awareness — no judgment, just observation. Ask yourself:

  • Do I tend to speak softly or quickly?
  • Do I over-explain or trail off?
  • Am I animated with gestures, or more still and internal?
  • Do I hold my breath or rush through my thoughts?
  • When I walk into a space, do I shrink or expand?

This is your current baseline — not good, not bad. Just your starting point. From here, you can make conscious shifts that align with how you want to show up and be experienced.

The Science of Non-Verbal Communication

Your body is always speaking — even when you’re silent. Research shows that over 70% of communication is non-verbal. That means your posture, eye contact, gestures, facial expressions, even how you stand or sit — all contribute to how your message is received.

Think of your body as your instrument. When you tune it with care, the world listens differently.

Power Poses vs. Protective Poses

Power Poses help you:

  • Breathe more fully
  • Ground your nervous system
  • Project calm confidence
  • Signal “I’m safe, I’m open, I’m present”

Try this before a meeting, pitch, or presentation:

  • Stand tall
  • Feet hip-width apart
  • Shoulders rolled down and back
  • Hands resting at your sides or gently on hips
  • Soft smile, open chest
  • Breathe deeply and say: “I belong here.”

Protective Poses (to become aware of, not judge):

  • Arms crossed tightly over the body
  • Shoulders hunched inward
  • Eyes darting or downward
  • Feet turned away from the group
  • Voice trailing or shrinking

These are usually unconscious responses to feeling unsafe. Simply noticing them is step one. Then, choose to shift.

Mastering the Subtleties of Tone

Your voice has layers: warmth, strength, intention, pace, inflection. You don’t have to change who you are — just align how you sound with what you mean.

Let’s practice this line:

“I’d love to share something I’ve been working on.”

  • Warm + Approachable: Soft tone, slight smile, slower pace — feels inviting and open.
  • Clear + Assertive: Grounded pace, confident energy, direct eye contact — feels trustworthy, composed.
  • Flat + Uncertain (what we’re releasing): Mumbled, rushed, no energy behind it — feels disconnected or hesitant.

Practice Tip: Record yourself saying it each way. Feel the difference in your body and energy. Which version feels most aligned with your truth?

Role Play: Embodied Confidence Practice

Partner A: Enters a mock “room” — as if arriving at a conference, an interview, or a networking event. They speak a brief introduction or simple opener: “Hi, I’m ___, and I’m passionate about __.”

Partner B: Observes posture, tone, eye contact, gestures. Reflects with compassionate feedback:

  • What felt aligned and authentic?
  • Where could there be a shift toward greater presence or power?
  • Was the voice warm, clear, and steady?

Bonus Practice

Record yourself, then review with curiosity and care. Notice your patterns. Celebrate your strengths. Gently invite tweaks. Micro-tweaks make a macro-impact.

Final Empowerment Reflection

“How I carry myself is not about performance — it’s about permission. I give myself permission to take up space, to speak clearly, to be seen.”

This is the heart of embodied communication. When you walk into a room next time — let your presence speak with clarity, kindness, and grounded power.
