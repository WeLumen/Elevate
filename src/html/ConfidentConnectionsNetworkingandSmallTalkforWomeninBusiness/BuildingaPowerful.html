Lesson 1: Building a Powerful, Authentic Introduction

“Your story is your superpower. Own it, shape it, speak it.”

Let’s be honest:

The moment someone asks, “So, what do you do?” …can send even the most brilliant humans into a flustered spiral of self-editing, over-explaining, or shrinking their light.

Why?
Because most people are trying to sound impressive instead of sounding real.

But here’s what truly connects:

  • Clarity over cleverness
  • Purpose over perfection
  • Being human over being polished

This final section is about crafting an introduction that feels like a mirror of your essence — not a résumé reel.

You’ll Learn How to…

  • Craft a powerful yet personal introduction that reflects both who you are and where you're going
  • Use the 3-Part Elevator Pitch Formula to create clarity and flow
  • Adapt your intro to different energies — from formal interviews to casual connections
  • Add a signature personal touch — that sparkle that makes people remember you
  • Walk away with a go-to script that feels like truth, not theater

The 3-Part Elevator Pitch Formula (Aligned + Authentic)

Let’s break it down, soul-first:

  1. Who You Are
    Start with identity — not just your job title, but the essence of how you move in the world.
    Tip: Lead with something you stand for or care about — this makes it personal, not transactional.
    Examples:
    “I’m a storyteller at heart, currently working as a content strategist…”
    “I’m someone who loves connecting people and ideas — my background in community building…”
    “I’m a designer who’s obsessed with making technology feel more human…”
  2. What You Do (or What Excites You)
    Describe your current work or a focus area that lights you up — even if you're still exploring.
    Keep it accessible, not jargon-filled. Speak with grounded passion.
    Examples:
    “Right now, I help nonprofits clarify their messaging and grow their reach.”
    “I’m developing a coaching program that helps women navigate big life pivots.”
    “I work in climate policy — lately I’ve been focused on local energy equity solutions.”
  3. Why It Matters (or the Value It Creates)
    This is the heart of your impact. Why does this work matter to you — and who does it serve?
    Let your values lead here. Speak from purpose.
    Examples:
    “Because I believe stories change minds — and movements need great messaging.”
    “It’s deeply rewarding to help people trust their own inner wisdom during transitions.”
    “I care about building systems that actually include everyone — especially in environmental decisions.”

Put It All Together — Example Intros

  • Formal / Interview Setting
    “I’m a project manager with a background in nonprofit operations, currently focused on streamlining team processes for mission-driven orgs. I’m passionate about helping values-based teams operate more smoothly so they can focus on the change they’re here to make.”
  • Casual / Coffee Chat
    “I’m a builder at heart — I love organizing chaos into clarity. Lately, that’s looked like helping nonprofits with team systems and workflows, but my real joy is being behind the scenes of people doing meaningful work.”
  • Networking / Creative Gathering
    “I’m a community-centered strategist with a soft spot for good stories and even better systems. I help small orgs build the scaffolding to support big missions.”

Add a Personal Touch

This is the sparkle — your signature.
Let something human shine through: a metaphor, a value, a hobby, or an “unusual” fact that makes you memorable.

  • “Outside of work, I run a monthly poetry circle. That’s where I refuel.”
  • “I always say I’m equal parts spreadsheet and soul — I love structure and intuition.”
  • “Fun fact: I’ve lived in four different countries — I think that’s where my love for bridging perspectives comes from.”

These small moments create instant connection.

Practice Time: Your Turn to Craft & Speak It

  • Draft your 3-part intro
  • Write multiple versions — formal, casual, creative. Play with tone, voice, rhythm.
  • Say it out loud. How does it feel to say it? Practice until it feels natural and embodied.
  • Try it in pairs or small groups. Give each other real-time feedback:
    • What felt most authentic?
    • Where did your energy light up?
    • What could be simplified or strengthened?
  • Record yourself. Play it back. Notice your posture, tone, and clarity. Tweak until it feels like you.

Empowerment Reflection:

“I’m not here to impress — I’m here to express. I don’t need to shrink, inflate, or perform. I just need to stand in my truth.”

When you speak from the soul, the right people lean in.
You don’t need a perfect pitch.
You just need one that’s rooted in who you are.

Identify strengths and blind spots in your communication

  • Understand your emotional response to visibility and social interaction
  • Set personal intentions for the course
  • This will serve as your baseline — so you can measure how far you grow.

Before you introduce yourself to the world, let’s check in with you.
This self-guided quiz helps you:

Scripted Introduction Using the 3-Part Formula

We use the 3-Part Authentic Intro Formula not to box you in — but to give your brilliance a container to flow through.
The Formula:

  • Who you are — Not just your job title. Speak from your essence.
  • What you do / what excites you — Speak in real language. Let your curiosity, creativity, or passion come through.
  • Why it matters — Anchor it in your values. Why does your work make a difference — even in a small way?

Example Intros with Soul + Strategy

  • Formal (e.g., interview, LinkedIn, panel intro)
    “Hi, I’m Amara. I’m a UX researcher who’s passionate about creating intuitive tech experiences for underrepresented users. I believe inclusive design can radically reshape who gets access to digital innovation.”
    Why it works: Anchored in identity + clarity; shows both what she does and why it matters; clear, grounded, and purpose-led.
  • Casual (e.g., coffee chat, creative space)
    “I’m Amara — I geek out on user experience and spend my days figuring out how apps can work better for real people. I’m especially excited about tech that’s designed with equity in mind.”
    Why it works: Feels personal and relaxed; “Real people” and “equity” make it memorable; invites curiosity instead of trying to impress.

Try This: Swap the Vibe of Your Intro

Now play with energy and tone. One message — different frequencies.

  • Version 1: Inspiring
    Bring warmth, vision, and heart.
    “I believe tech should feel like an invitation — not a barrier. That’s why I design with empathy and equity at the core.”
  • Version 2: Concise
    For quick intros, first impressions, or fast-paced spaces.
    “I’m a UX researcher focused on inclusive design — making tech more intuitive and accessible.”
  • Version 3: Assertive
    For when you need to claim space confidently.
    “I lead research that ensures digital tools actually serve marginalized communities. It’s not just about function — it’s about fairness.”

Notice how each version serves a different purpose. You don’t have to pick just one. You’re allowed to be dynamic.

Role Play Prompt: Real-Time Reflection + Reframe

  • Partner A:
    Delivers their intro as if in a formal setting (interview, networking, etc.)
  • Partner B:
    Offers supportive feedback:
    What stood out?
    What felt clear and true?
    What felt rehearsed or distant?
    Then gently asks:
    “How would you say that if we were chatting casually at a coffee shop?”

Why this matters:
Because your message should live in your body, not just your brain. If it only sounds good on paper but doesn’t feel natural, we shift until it does.

Try swapping:
Formal → Casual
Inspiring → Concise
Casual → Assertive

Bonus: Record yourself. Watch your body language, tone, and eyes. Ask: Do I sound like me? If not — simplify, soften, and try again.

Final Empowerment Reflection:

“When I introduce myself, I’m not auditioning — I’m aligning. I’m not proving — I’m revealing. My presence is enough.”

Your voice is a vessel for truth.
Let it carry your story with clarity, care, and quiet power.

Personal Communication Style Quiz

Discover Your Archetype: The Path to Authentic Expression

Every woman has a natural communication rhythm — a unique frequency that, when honored, becomes her superpower. This quiz isn’t about boxing you in. It’s about giving language to the energy you already carry — so you can move through the world with self-trust and strategic clarity.

“The most magnetic women are the ones who’ve stopped trying to fit someone else’s mold.”

When you know your communication archetype, you’re better equipped to:

  • Play to your strengths
  • Spot where you may be dimming your light
  • Tailor your connection strategies with more authenticity and ease

Let’s explore the archetypes:

The Connector

Essence: You thrive on warmth, openness, and shared energy. People feel safe and seen around you.

Strengths: Empathy, relationship-building, emotional intelligence

Watch-for: People-pleasing, over-agreeing, avoiding disagreement

Networking Tip: Lean into deep 1:1 conversations. You shine when you genuinely care — so don’t chase the crowd, find the connection.

“I build bridges through presence, not performance.”

The Storyteller

Essence: You speak in imagery, emotion, and narrative. You draw people in through the heart, not just the facts.

Strengths: Emotionally resonant, memorable, captivating

Watch-for: Going off-track, avoiding clarity, over-sharing

Networking Tip: Have 2–3 anchor stories that show who you are, what you value, or how you got here — then shape them for context.

“My story is my strategy.”

The Strategist

Essence: You value clarity, logic, and precision. Your words have weight and intention.

Strengths: Structure, efficiency, articulating complex ideas

Watch-for: Sounding too formal, rigid, or distant; holding back warmth

Networking Tip: Add a personal detail or “why it matters” to your message. Your strength is your mind — but your magnetism is your why.

“I don’t just speak — I shape meaning.”

The Listener

Essence: You’re attuned. You notice what others miss. People feel deeply understood in your presence.

Strengths: Intuition, emotional safety, active listening

Watch-for: Staying too quiet, undervaluing your voice, blending into the background

Networking Tip: Ask beautiful questions — then add your insight. Your power isn’t in speaking first — it’s in speaking with precision and care.

“My silence is powerful. So is my voice.”

Remember:
You’re not “just one” archetype — you’re a dynamic blend. But knowing your primary style helps you lean in with confidence and lead from alignment, not anxiety.
