Section 5: Optional Group Discussion (for Group Courses or Workshops)

Let’s take a sacred pause and create space for connection and truth-telling.

Invite your students to share in small groups or a full-circle share-out. Encourage vulnerability and reflection — this is where transformation takes root.

Prompt 1: A Limiting Belief They're Shifting About Speaking Up or Being Seen

“What’s one belief you’ve been carrying about being visible or vocal that no longer serves you — and what are you choosing to believe instead?”

This opens the door for honesty. It’s where old stories begin to unravel, and new, empowered narratives start to take form.

Examples:
  • “I used to think I had to be perfect before I could speak — now I know progress is more powerful than perfection.”
  • “I believed people would judge me if I took up space — now I know my voice can be a bridge for others.”

Prompt 2: A Go-To Phrase or Mantra for Networking Moments

“What phrase, mantra, or energetic cue will you take with you into your next networking or visibility opportunity?”

This is about anchoring into intention. Words are powerful — they shape how we show up.

Examples:
  • “I belong in every room I enter.”
  • “Confidence is a muscle — I’m strengthening it with every step.”
  • “I’m not here to prove, I’m here to connect.”

Prompt 3: A Success Story or Real-Life Breakthrough

“Have you experienced a shift, even a small one, by applying what we’ve practiced? Celebrate it!”

This is your moment to witness each other’s growth. Success leaves clues — and even small wins deserve a standing ovation.

Examples:
  • “I spoke up in a team meeting and actually enjoyed it!”
  • “I made eye contact and smiled at three new people at an event — and one turned into a real connection!”
  • “I rewrote my LinkedIn bio and actually feel seen in it.”

Scripted Internal Dialogue + Reframe

Let’s go deeper. The way we speak to ourselves shapes the way we move in the world.

Limiting Belief:
“I don’t want to come off too pushy.”
Energetic Root:

Fear of rejection, fear of disrupting harmony, people-pleasing patterns.

Reframe (Empowered Truth):
“Sharing my ideas isn’t pushy — it’s generous. My voice carries wisdom and intention. When I speak up, I create possibilities.”

This is not about taking space away from others — it’s about making space for your truth.

Example Before:
“Sorry, this might be a dumb idea, but maybe we could… uh, try something different?”
Example After (with grounded confidence):
“I’ve been reflecting on something — what if we approached this from a fresh angle? Here’s what I’m envisioning…”
Optional Body Cue:

Invite them to take a breath, roll their shoulders back, and say it again — but this time with presence. Let their voice land.

Role Play Prompt: Empowerment Through Coaching

We grow fastest when we’re seen and supported in the community.

Partner A (shares a limiting belief):
“I’m just really bad at small talk — I always feel awkward.”
Partner B (coaches and uplifts):

First, mirror and validate. “That makes so much sense — small talk can feel unnatural when we’re craving real connection.”

Then, reframe. “What if you didn’t need to be ‘good’ at small talk — just curious and kind?”

Suggest a micro-action — a small, doable step that builds confidence.

“What if you picked three openers that feel like you? Like: ‘What’s something you’re looking forward to this month?’ or ‘What’s a passion project you’re working on?’”

Encourage each partner to practice one of their openers aloud — not to get it “right,” but to feel how it shifts their energy.
Let it be playful. Let it be human.

Nice work! This Lesson is complete.
