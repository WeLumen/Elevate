Lesson 1: The Era of Streaming

Streaming as a Tool to Connect Different Generations

Streaming and live video have become powerful tools for brands to connect with different generations, ranging from the youngest, like Generation Z, to older generations such as Millennials and Boomers. An effective brand strategy involves adapting to the live content consumption habits of each generation, creating personalised and authentic experiences.

Steps for an Effective Strategy:

1. Choosing the Right Platform for Each Generation

The first step is to select the streaming platforms that best match the interests and behaviours of different generations:

  • Generation Z: Highly active on Twitch and the live streaming features of TikTok and Instagram. These users seek dynamic and entertaining content with strong real-time interaction. The ideal format for them includes live broadcasts that encourage participation through comments, live polls, or collaborations with popular creators.
  • Millennials: Frequent users of platforms like YouTube for live streaming, and they also use Instagram Live. They value educational content and live experiences that provide value or useful information, such as tutorials, interviews, and discussions on topics of interest.
  • Generation X and Boomers: Although less active on platforms like Twitch, they tend to use Facebook Live for live events, as they look for more traditional experiences. To connect with them, a more in-depth and explanatory content strategy can be effective, such as Q&A sessions or live product presentations.

2. Creating Content that Connects Across Generations

Live content should be designed with the potential to appeal to multiple generations simultaneously. Some ideas include:

  • Live Product Demonstrations: This type of content is relevant to all generations. For example, a tech brand could demonstrate a new gadget while answering questions live. Younger audiences might appreciate the innovation and immediacy, while older generations value detailed information and direct interaction with the brand.
  • Storytelling Events: Younger generations value authenticity, while older consumers appreciate the story behind a business. Creating a live stream where the company founder shares the brand’s story can be an excellent way to engage both audiences.
  • Live Competitions or Giveaways: These drive engagement across all age groups. Younger viewers connect for the entertainment factor, while older generations are drawn to the promotional aspects.

3. Collaborating with Intergenerational Influencers

Collaborations in live video with influencers help attract different audiences. The key is to select influencers who embody the brand’s values and connect with multiple generations. For instance:

  • A younger influencer could attract Generation Z on platforms like Twitch or TikTok.
  • A lifestyle or entrepreneurship micro-influencer could appeal to Millennials and Generation X on YouTube or Instagram Live.

4. Providing Interactive Experiences That Build Community

Younger generations seek dynamic and interactive experiences, while older ones value community building. A brand can host live events such as:

  • Live Workshopsor Classes that teach something useful to everyone (e.g., an organic food brand could host a live cooking session with a chef).
  • Q&A Sessions where customers of all ages can interact with the brand in real-time.
  • Live Product Launches, where customers can watch unboxings, test the product, and share their feedback live.

5. Leveraging Data to Segment Live Content

Streaming platforms allow real-time data collection on who is watching the content. Brands can use this information to tailor future live broadcasts to their audience's preferences, segmenting by age, interests, or consumption habits. This enables personalised content for each generation’s preferences

Example

Imagine a brand that sells sustainable home decor products. It could:

  • Host an Instagram Live in collaboration with an interior design influencer (appealing to Millennials and Generation X).
  • Launch a live tutorial on TikTok for decorating small spaces with a modern, eco-friendly touch, targeting Generation Z.
  • Then, hold a Facebook Live session with a detailed demonstration of the quality of its sustainable products, aimed at an older audience.

By implementing these actions, the brand would be using the power of streaming and live video to connect effectively with various generations, adapting to their preferences and ways of engaging with live content.
