Lesson 4: Interaction and Engagement

Before thinking about how we can generate interaction, let’s consider what consumers trust.

What Do Consumers Trust?

Online Consumer Opinions

Reviews from other consumers are the second most trusted source. Around 70% of respondents trust user reviews posted online, showing a significant increase in trust over the years. This type of content generates a perception of authenticity and transparency that traditional forms of advertising often fail to achieve.

This is why influencer content works.

This is why we, as a brand, should focus on generating earned media—conversations where third parties speak positively about us.

Recommendations from Known People

According to Nielsen, the most trusted source remains word-of-mouth or recommendations from friends and family. Around 92% of global consumers trust this type of “earned media,” showing that personal connections are still essential in influencing purchasing decisions in an increasingly saturated digital environment.

Social Media Advertising

Although less trusted than recommendations and reviews, ads on social media have been gaining ground. Approximately 36% of consumers trust sponsored ads on platforms like Facebook and Instagram, showing a growing trend toward the acceptance of digital advertising.

Content on Brand Websites

Official brand websites also generate a relatively high level of trust, with 58% of consumers indicating that they trust the information they find there. This indicates that brands still have control over how they communicate their message and can leverage their own channels to strengthen trust.


Therefore, we should be able to create a mix of all these content types, prioritizing those that can emotionally engage users.

And What Can We Do to Ensure Content Spreads Effectively in This Way?

The factors that contribute to ideas spreading effectively among people include:

They Resonate with People’s Emotions

Ideas that evoke strong emotions, whether positive or negative, are more likely to be shared. When an idea connects emotionally, people feel the urge to share it with others.

They Offer Value or Benefit

Ideas that provide tangible value or solve a specific problem for people are more likely to spread. If an idea improves someone's life, they will want to share it.

They Are Easy to Understand and Share

Simplicity is key. Clear and concise ideas are easier to convey. If an idea is complicated, people may feel frustrated and less inclined to share it.

They Generate Conversation

Ideas that spark discussion or debate encourage people to talk about them. When an idea is interesting or novel, it becomes a topic of conversation, which extends its reach.

They Allow People to Feel Part of Something Bigger

If an idea makes people feel connected to a community or movement, they are more likely to adopt and spread it. The sense of belonging drives the spread.

They Are Credible and Trustworthy

Ideas backed by evidence or coming from trustworthy sources are more likely to be accepted and shared. Trust is fundamental in the diffusion of ideas.

They Spread Through the Right Channels

Using the right media and platforms helps ensure the idea reaches the target audience. Adapting the message to the appropriate channel maximizes its impact.


In other words, having a good idea is not enough; it is essential to present it in a way that motivates people to share it. For an idea to spread, it must be relevant, emotionally resonant, and easy to convey.

That's why it is important to understand the motivations and know our audience, as it will be easier to create content they want to share.

Active Audience Participation

For a social media strategy to be truly effective, it is essential to invite and encourage users to participate actively. This is achieved not only through engaging content but also by giving them a reason to get involved.

How to Increase Interaction with the Brand?

Good content should not only capture attention but also encourage action. To achieve this, it is important to create an authentic connection with the audience and give them clear reasons to interact:

  • Calls to Action (CTA): Use clear and direct prompts to encourage the user to take action, such as “comment”, “share”, “join our contest”, or “find out more here”.
  • Emotional Rewards: Users who feel part of a community or movement are more inclined to support and recommend a brand. Therefore, content that fosters a sense of belonging or emotion tends to be more effective.
  • Rational Motivation: An example might be offering tangible incentives like discounts, special promotions, or exclusive access to certain content or products.

How Can We Include Elements in Our Tactics to Stimulate Conversation?

It is important to understand that the more elements we incorporate, the easier it will be for our audiences to want to interact with and share what we create. Here is a checklist—if four of these elements are included in our tactical approach, we can be confident that it will likely be shared.

Do we have a good story?

Stories generate emotions and are easy to remember and share. A compelling narrative connects with people and motivates them to talk about it.

Can people show their involvement visibly?

When users can showcase their participation, such as by using products or sharing experiences, they become natural ambassadors of the brand.

Do we offer something new or exclusive to talk about?

Novelty always grabs attention. If you offer something innovative or limited, people will want to be the first to talk about it.

Do we allow our followers to be creative?

Allowing users to personalise or create their own content encourages active participation and a sense of belonging.

Do we invite people to participate?

Direct invitations, like contests or challenges, encourage people to get involved and share.

Do we offer them something of value?

Value can be functional (discounts, information) or emotional (satisfaction, entertainment), motivating users to share.

Do we remind people to share?

A simple reminder or call to action may be all that is needed to prompt users to spread the message.

Generating Emotional Impact

When brands tap into deep emotions (such as happiness or surprise), users are more inclined to share the content.
