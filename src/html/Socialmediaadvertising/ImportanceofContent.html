Lesson 1: Importance of Content

The first question we should ask ourselves is, what is content?

Content is the collection of messages, photographs, creative pieces, mobile applications, infographics, and any type of multimedia that we create first and then distribute with the objective we have set, which can be building a brand, communicating its value proposition, or generating interaction with its communities, among others.

Content always has two perspectives.

What is content used for on social media?

From the Consumer's Perspective:

  • To seek general information
  • To entertain themselves
  • To learn about a particular topic
  • To make decisions (purchases, training, work-related)
  • To communicate and converse with others
  • To purchase products and services
  • To carry out administrative tasks, whether in the workplace or personally

From the Companies' Perspective:

  • To conquer territories of conversation
  • To engage and define the user/customer experience
  • To create information about their products and services
  • To generate marketing and advertising activities
  • To communicate, create identity, and build brand reputation
  • To associate the brand with specific attributes
  • To facilitate customer service

Content Strategy

Given the complexity of content within the company’s ecosystem, it is essential that it has a strategic approach.

Quoting Kristina Halvorson, CEO of Brain Traffic, the content strategy is:

“It is the discipline that plans the creation, publication, and governance of useful and usable content.”

Every company wanting to engage in social media must have a clear content strategy approved by all departments.
