Lesson 2: The Path to Technology Adoption

Now that we have defined and examined the importance of digital transformation and its use across industries, let's explore it through five pillars:

  • The Internet of Things (IoT)
  • Cloud
  • Artificial intelligence (AI)
  • Cybersecurity
  • Blockchain

In the video below, Dr. Abel Sanchez provides an introduction to the five pillars of digital transformation, beginning with an overview of IoT.

Internet of Things

As Dr. Sanchez discusses in the video, the Internet of Things (IoT) starts with connectivity and devices. IoT devices are equipped with sensors and connectivity capabilities such as Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and cellular data to collect and exchange data over the Internet

Two ways that IoT collects and exchanges data over the Internet are via edge computing, which enables real-time analysis at the source of data generation, and predictive maintenance, which utilizes this data to foresee and prevent equipment failures.

Edge computing is the practice of processing, analyzing, and storing data closer to where it is produced, rather than sending the data to a central location. In edge computing, data is processed locally on edge devices or gateways, which are closer to the IoT devices generating the data.

For example, imagine you have a smart security camera installed outside your home. This camera is designed to detect motion and send you alerts whenever it detects any movement. Instead of sending all the video footage to a remote cloud server for analysis, the camera utilizes edge computing to process some of the data locally. The smart security camera therefore reduces the need to continuously send large amounts of video data to the cloud, conserving bandwidth and reducing latency.

Predictive maintenance is a strategy that uses data and analytics to know when components of a machine or device need to be changed. Predicative maintenance is used for sensor data collection, which is when sensors are installed on a machine to continuously monitor parameters such as temperature, current, and pressure, and gather real-time data about the health and performance of machines.

For example, imagine you have a commercial building with multiple air conditioning units responsible for maintaining a comfortable indoor temperature. These units are equipped with sensors to monitor various aspects of their operation. These sensors gather data including parameters like temperature, pressure, compressor performance, and energy consumption. In this scenario, the predictive maintenance process involves data collection from sensors, analysis for anomalies, alert generation, and timely maintenance scheduling to ensure the reliable and efficient operation of air conditioning units.


How does IoT Impact Your Organization?

Today, every organization and business that is undergoing a digital transformation needs to make decisions based on data. When there is more data at an organization's disposal, better decisions can be made.

To get the data, there is a pyramid of people needed.

Machine Learning Engineers

Machine learning algorithms require large amounts of data to learn patterns and make accurate predictions. Humans are needed to collect and curate IoT data to train algorithms used in machine learning.

Data Analysts

Data analysts are needed to extract valuable insights from data, support decision-making processes, and provide essential context to data engineers and stakeholders.

Data Engineers

Data engineers can be seen as building the nervous system of an organization. They ensure that the data is robust, efficient, and properly prepared for training and interference.

Cloud

The next pillar of digital transformation is cloud computing. The term “cloud” encompasses diverse definitions; nevertheless, a clearer comprehension of its essence and capabilities can be achieved by examining its fundamental components.

The cloud’s infrastructure provides virtualized computing resources like servers and storage.

The platform offers tools and environments for application development and deployment. Platform as a service (PaaS) provides a platform and environment for developers to build, deploy, and manage applications without having to worry about the underlying infrastructure.

This is an approach where a combination of both public and private cloud services and private cloud infrastructure are used to manage its data, applications, and workloads. This allows businesses to take advantage of the scalability and cost-effectiveness of public clouds while retaining control of sensitive data and critical applications in their private cloud or on-premises environment.

Cloud storage services provide scalable and accessible storage solutions over the Internet. Users can store and retrieve data from anywhere.

Let’s put everything together with an example.

Imagine “Global Bank,” a forward-looking bank aiming to enhance its services using cloud computing. This digital transformation involves creating a powerful online banking hub (infrastructure), assembling ready-made tools for seamless operations (platform), and incorporating features like video calls and secure data storage (cloud storage). By adapting effortlessly to changing demands (scalability) and fortifying against cyber threats (security), Global Bank ensures only authorized users can access the system (security) and learns from customer behavior to refine services (analytics). This cloud-powered makeover propels Global Bank toward efficient, secure, and customer-centric banking experiences.

Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence (AI) has emerged as a pivotal force driving the profound evolution of digital transformation, reshaping industries and enabling unprecedented levels of efficiency, innovation, and customer engagement.

In the video below, Dr. Abel Sanchez describes the link between the cloud and artificial intelligence, and how companies are utilizing AI to gain a competitive advantage in the digital transformation process.

As Dr. Sanchez described in the video, AI capabilities are connected to cloud computing as they provide the necessary infrastructure and computing resources that are essential for running AI algorithms and models.

For example, many e-commerce platforms use AI algorithms hosted on the cloud infrastructure to provide personalized shopping recommendations for their customers. An e-commerce platform might use AI to examine a customer's past purchases, items they've viewed, and products they've liked or reviewed. The AI system then processes this information in the cloud and compares it with data from millions of other shoppers to identify patterns and trends. Based on this analysis, the system can generate personalized product recommendations that are displayed to the customer when they visit the website or app.

Cloud computing is crucial in this scenario as it provides the scalable and powerful infrastructure needed to process and analyze vast amounts of data from various sources in real time. This combination of AI and cloud computing in e-commerce not only enhances customer engagement but also increases sales and customer satisfaction by presenting shoppers with products that align closely with their preferences and behaviors.

Now, let’s look at some steps your organization can take to leverage AI capabilities in your digital transformation journe

1. Identify Business Goals

Understand the specific business goals and objectives that digital transformation aims to achieve. Determine how AI can contribute to these goals, such as enhancing customer service, optimizing operations, or creating new revenue streams.

2. AI Readiness Assessment

Evaluate the organization's current AI capabilities, infrastructure, and data. Identify potential gaps and areas where AI can provide the most value

3. Develop AI Strategy

Formulate a clear AI strategy that aligns with the overall digital transformation roadmap. Define the scope of AI adoption, target use cases, and expected outcomes.

4. Choose Appropriate AI Techniques

Select the right AI techniques for the identified use cases. This could include machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and more.

5. Cloud Services

Leverage cloud platforms for AI model deployment and hosting. Cloud services provide scalability and flexibility, making it easier to deploy and manage AI solutions.

By following these steps, organizations can effectively leverage AI capabilities to drive their digital transformation initiatives, enhance operational efficiency, create new value propositions, and improve customer experiences.

Cybersecurity

In today's interconnected world, and with our reliance on digital infrastructure, organizations that lack strong security measures risk compromising their entire foundation. As organizations undergo digital transformation, it becomes crucial for them to recognize, tackle, and adapt to cybersecurity risks.

Developing a cybersecurity framework can be divided into three phases:

Prevention is the first phase in designing a holistic cybersecurity framework. Prevention deals with identifying and creating plans for threats and risks before they occur, dealing with breaches that have occurred, and continuously monitoring, reviewing, and revising risk and action plans. Prevention also involves risk mapping, action planning, training and development, and employee monitoring.

The second phase in the development of a holistic cybersecurity framework is monitoring, which is sometimes referred to as detection. Monitoring involves mechanisms being put in place for the early detection of cyberattacks. Steps to be taken in the monitoring phase include the control and collection of data, IT governance, and ensuring that all systems are kept up-to-date with automatic updates.

The last phase of developing a holistic cybersecurity approach is the reaction phase, i.e., the establishment of a rapid response system with strategies for containing damage and returning the organization to normal after a cyberattack occurs. The reaction phase includes creating action plans to ensure that everyone on an organizational level knows how to react in the event of a cyberattack.


Remember, cybersecurity is an ongoing process and while this plan provides a solid foundation, it's important to adapt it to your organization's unique needs and circumstances. Regularly reassess and enhance your cybersecurity measures as technology and threats evolve.

Blockchain

The last pillar of digital transformation that we will discuss is blockchain.

Blockchain is a global peer-to-peer network enabling decentralized data transactions where anyone can contribute and secure data through a chain of transactions. It uses consensus mechanisms as its foundational technology.

Blockchain consists of three properties: identity, cryptocurrency, and smart contracts.




Blockchain technology can provide a secure and decentralized way to manage digital identities. With a blockchain-based identity system, individuals or entities can have their identity information stored securely on the blockchain. Users have control over their own identity and can share specific information with others as needed, enhancing privacy and reducing the risk of identity theft.

For example, imagine a person wants to prove their age to access a certain online service. With blockchain, an individual can create an identity for themselves with verified information such as their age and name. This information can also be stored across multiple computers on the blockchain network.




Cryptocurrency is a digital form of currency that uses cryptographic techniques to secure transactions and control the creation of new units. Many cryptocurrencies, like Bitcoin and Ethereum, are built on blockchain technology. In a blockchain-based cryptocurrency system, transactions are recorded on the blockchain, creating a transparent and immutable ledger.

The decentralized nature of the blockchain ensures that transactions can be conducted directly between users without the need for intermediaries like banks. This can lead to faster and more efficient cross-border transactions and increased financial inclusion.




Smart contracts are self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement directly written into code. These contracts automatically execute and enforce themselves when predefined conditions are met. Blockchain technology enables the deployment of smart contracts, which are stored and executed on the blockchain.

They can facilitate a wide range of transactions, from financial agreements to supply chain management and more. Smart contracts increase efficiency by automating processes and reducing the need for intermediaries. They also enhance transparency and trust, as all parties can verify the contract's execution on the public blockchain.


In summary, blockchain technology offers a decentralized and secure framework for managing digital identities, enables the creation of cryptocurrencies with transparent and efficient transactions, and empowers the execution of self-executing smart contracts for a variety of applications.
