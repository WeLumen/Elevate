Lesson 1: Key Areas of Digitization

In this section, we'll explore vital aspects of digitization that organizations must identify and adopt in their digital transformation journey. This encompasses essential areas for digitization and optimizing core processes for effective outcomes.

Optimizing Core Processes

As highlighted in the video, organizations prioritize cybersecurity, data gathering, cloud computing, automation, and seeking guidance from a digital transformation advisor. Now, let's look at the tangible steps and strategies organizations can take to optimize their core processes.

1. Process Assessment

Organizations should begin by thoroughly assessing existing core processes to identify inefficiencies and pain points. This involves understanding the end-to-end workflows, identifying bottlenecks, and determining areas for improvement.

2. Digital Mapping

Organizations may opt to develop comprehensive digital maps of their core processes. These maps aid in pinpointing opportunities for technology integration, resulting in improved efficiency and streamlined operations. Digital mapping is a visual process that enables organizations to visualize their workflows, identify dependencies between steps, and pinpoint integration points.

3. Agile Implementation

Organizations should adopt an agile approach to implementing changes. It also helps to break down the transformation into manageable phases, allowing for iterative improvements and quick adjustments based on feedback.

4. Employee Training and Engagement

Organizations must provide thorough training for employees to ensure they are comfortable with new technologies and processes. They should engage them in the transformation journey by highlighting the benefits and involving them in decision-making.

5. Feedback Loop

It is important that organizations establish a continuous feedback loop to gather insights from employees, customers, and other stakeholders. This feedback can be used to drive further improvements and fine-tune the optimized core processes.


By following these steps and maintaining a proactive, adaptive approach, organizations can successfully optimize their core processes during the digital transformation journey, leading to increased efficiency, agility, and competitiveness.


Creating a Digital Transformation Strategy

For the digital transformation process to be successful, it is key to have an integrated strategy with clear transformation goals. Research has shown that organizations with an integrated approach increase their likelihood of success in the process.

Business use cases serve as a valuable tool to outline the interaction between people and a proposed strategy or solution. Particularly in the context of digital transformation, business use cases play a crucial role in facilitating the integration of digital technologies within a business framework. For example, if a business wants to incorporate an employee onboarding system, the business will create a business use case to highlight its objectives, main flow of events in integration, benefits, and key performance indicators.

After the business use cases are created, it is important to ensure that flexible tech and data platforms are put into place and align with the business use cases.

Digital transformation requires a change or shift in the culture of the business. This includes having an organizational structure of leadership. In this phase, employees at all levels must be trained to use the technologies that will be implemented.

The last phase includes creating a roadmap that ensures the strategy is being implemented correctly at all levels. This is accomplished by developing a change management plan and a communication plan to ensure that all roles are clearly defined and being executed.
