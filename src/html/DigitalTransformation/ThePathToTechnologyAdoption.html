Lesson 1: The Path to Technology Adoption

To gain a better understanding of what digital transformation is, it is useful to look at how technology creation and adoption has impacted our everyday life.

Let's consider the example of the telephone. Since its introduction, the telephone has followed an adoption curve that has gradually evolved over time. This progression indicates a notable improvement in its capabilities. The journey of telephones began with traditional landline models plugged into walls, and has now advanced to the smartphone, which was introduced more than a decade ago.

Today the smartphone, which essentially functions as a computer, has more adoption in a shorter amount of time than the landline telephone did. The smartphone contains the same capabilities as a computer when it comes to computing, storage, and connectivity.

Like the telephone, technology is being adopted faster than ever today. Technology that used to take years to create and adopt is now being adopted at faster speeds and efficiency. The example of the telephone shows how digital transformation has reached all areas of society on a global scale.

Understanding Digital Transformation: Definition and Concept

Let's explore the concept of digital transformation and why businesses and organizations across all sectors are beginning to implement this phenomenon.

The concept of digital transformation represents the strategic adoption of digital technologies to improve processes and productivity, manage business risks, and improve customer service (Citrix, 2018). The goal of digital transformation is to leverage technology to create innovative solutions and stay competitive in a rapidly evolving digital world.

Some of the top reasons companies choose to go through the process of digital transformation include:

Enhanced customer experience

Digital transformation allows businesses to offer more personalized experiences to their customers, meeting their evolved expectations and demands.

Increased efficiency and productivity

By integrating digital tools and automation, businesses can streamline processes, reduce costs, and improve overall operational efficiency.

Data-driven decision-making

Digital transformation enables data collection and analysis, empowering businesses and organizations to make informed and strategic decisions based on real-time data. .

New business models

Digital transformation creates opportunities for businesses to explore new revenue streams and business models that were not possible before the digital era.

Today, gaining a competitive advantage through digital transformation relies on several key drivers...

Connected Devices

Connected devices, also known as the Internet of Things (IoT), are physical objects or devices that are equipped with embedded sensors, software, and network connectivity, enabling them to exchange data and interact with other devices and systems over the Internet.

Examples of connected devices include smartphones and tablets, smart cars, smart home devices, and wearable devices such as smartwatches.

Cloud technology

Cloud technologyis the delivery of computing services, such as storage, databases, software, and processing power, through the Internet.

Cloud technology plays a crucial role in digital transformation as it offers numerous benefits that drive innovation, efficiency, and flexibility within organizations.

Advantages of using cloud technology include scalability and flexibility, cost efficiency, and data storage and management.

Integrated systems

Integrated systems are the implementation of IoT technologies and software platforms that work together seamlessly to optimize an organization’s processes and data management.

For example, an enterprise resource planning (ERP) system integrates different business functions like finance, human resources, manufacturing, and sales into a single platform, allowing data to flow smoothly between departments.

Digital Transformation: Industry Examples

As we have seen in the previous section, digital transformation is being embraced by nearly all organizations to enhance their overall processes and operations. Now, let's delve into how digital transformation manifests itself across various industries.

In the video below, Dr. Abel Sanchez discusses how the agricultural industry is experiencing rapid growth in terms of digital transformation.

The agricultural industry is not the only industry experiencing rapid growth due to the implementation of digital transformation. In fact, various sectors, such as manufacturing, health care, finance, and retail, are also witnessing significant advancements and improvements through this transformative process. Let’s take a closer look at digital transformation in the sports, education, and health care industries.

Sports

The sports industry embraces digital transformation for growth opportunities. They are pursuing this strategy for several reasons, including expanding their content reach, driving the fan experience, generating revenue, and improving player and team performance.

The American National Football League (NFL) is an example of a digital transformation in process. Players’ equipment, such as shoulder pads, comes equipped with an RFID chip to collect data from players’ movements on the field. The data is then used to improve individual and team performance.

Education

The education sector is undergoing a shift with digital transformation, adapting traditional teaching methods to accommodate digitized technology and cater to diverse learner needs. While traditional forms of education almost completely evolved around in-person classroom learning, today there are other methods of instruction and learning such as remote and hybrid learning formats.

The digitization of education expands offerings and opportunities, including certificate programs and professional development outside traditional university degree programs. Additionally, online learning has allowed people from all over the world to take classes outside of limited geographical areas.

Health care

The health care industry increasingly relies on digital technology to enhance health care delivery and address medical issues. An example of this is the implementation of electronic health records (EHRs). EHRs replace traditional paper-based patient records with electronic systems. With EHRs, health care providers can access a patient’s medical history, lab results, prescriptions, and other relevant information quickly and securely. EHRs also facilitate data collection on a large scale, which can be anonymized and used for research, health management, and identifying trends or patterns in diseases and treatments.
