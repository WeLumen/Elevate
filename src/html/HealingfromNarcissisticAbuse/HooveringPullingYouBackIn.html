Lesson 11: Hoovering & Pulling You Back In

What it looks like

Imagine weeks or even months of silence, where you’ve barely heard a word from them. Suddenly, out of the blue, you receive a message or call. They’re sweet, apologetic, almost desperate. They say things like, “I’ve changed,” “I’m so sorry for hurting you,” or “Remember how amazing we were together?” This is hoovering—a term borrowed from the vacuum cleaner because they are trying to suck you back into the exhausting cycle of the relationship.

They may flood your inbox with texts, send gifts, or even show up unexpectedly, claiming they want to make things right. It feels confusing because deep down, a small part of you remembers those “good times” they mention, but the rest of you recalls the emotional chaos, the gaslighting, the heartbreak.

Real-life example

As a coach and survivor, I’ve seen clients describe this perfectly: After weeks of silence, the narcissist suddenly texts at 2 a.m., saying, “I miss you so much. I can’t sleep without you. I promise I’ll never hurt you again.” This triggers an emotional whirlwind—hope, pain, fear—all at once. But when they do reconnect, the pattern of manipulation soon returns.

One client shared how her ex would apologize profusely and promise to change, only to revert to the same controlling behaviors within days. He’d talk about their “beautiful memories,” trying to convince her that their relationship was special and worth fighting for, even though she felt drained and unseen.

A gentle reminder

This cycle only ends when you decide it does. You have the power. Healing begins the moment you realize their sweet words are just that—words without action. Promises without change are empty.

When you choose your peace over their chaos, you reclaim your life. You stop walking back into emotional traps that hurt you.

Your Path to Healing and Empowerment

Dear beautiful soul,

The journey you are on is nothing short of remarkable. I know that the road to recovery after narcissistic abuse can feel overwhelming and uncertain, but I want you to take a moment to honor yourself for having the strength and courage to take that first step. Healing is not an overnight process, but every small effort you make to reconnect with your true self is a powerful act of self-love, bravery, and resilience.

Narcissistic abuse has caused deep wounds, leaving you to question your worth and your ability to trust yourself and others. However, you are so much more than the pain you have experienced. The abuse you endured does not define you—it has merely been one chapter in your story, and now you are stepping into a new, empowered, and healed version of yourself.

As your holistic coach, I want you to know that healing is a journey of rediscovery. It's about reconnecting with your body, mind, and spirit—taking the time to nurture each part of yourself. Along the way, you will rediscover your strength, reclaim your self-worth, and learn to set boundaries that honor and protect your inner peace. You have the power to rebuild your life, and you already hold within you the wisdom, courage, and resilience to do so.

Every step you take towards healing, no matter how small, is an important victory. There is no rush—be gentle with yourself. Take the time you need to process the emotional, mental, and physical toll that narcissistic abuse has taken on you. Remember, healing is not linear. Some days will feel lighter, while others may feel heavier. That’s okay. What matters is that you continue to move forward, even if just a little bit at a time.

You are worthy of love, respect, and kindness, and I want to remind you that this journey is about reclaiming what is rightfully yours: your peace, your joy, your power, and your authenticity. There is a life waiting for you that is full of the beauty you may have thought was lost— a life where you can trust, love, and fully embrace yourself once again.

The healing process may require patience and effort, but I know that you are more than capable of navigating this path.

As we move through the process of healing, it’s important to cultivate deep self-compassion. Understand that it’s normal to feel a mixture of emotions as you recover. Some days you might feel like you’ve taken several steps forward, and other days you may feel as though you’ve taken a step back. But remember, every emotion you feel is a step toward wholeness— there is no shame in the ebbs and flows of healing. You have every right to honor your emotions and give yourself the space to fully process them.

By learning to trust and set boundaries, you will also build your confidence and reinforce your worth. You have already come so far, and each day you are getting closer to the new, empowered version of yourself—the version of you that shines brightly without fear, the one who knows what she or he deserves and is ready to live life fully, authentically, and joyfully.

I want you to always remember: you are not alone in this process. As your holistic coach, I am here to guide you, support you, and remind you of your inherent strength and potential every step of the way. Together, we will create a roadmap for your healing—one that honors your unique journey and empowers you to live in alignment with your highest self.

It’s time to release the grip that the past has had on you. You no longer have to carry the emotional weight of the narcissist’s behavior. You deserve to be free, to live in peace, and to experience love in ways that are healthy, reciprocal, and honoring of who you truly are. Healing from narcissistic abuse is not about forgetting what happened, but rather about reclaiming your power, your voice, and your life.

The future holds so much potential for you. It holds deep healing, self-empowerment, and a life filled with authentic, loving relationships that nourish your soul. You can step into a life that reflects your highest values and desires. Trust the process—your healing has already begun, and each new day is an opportunity to get closer to the life you deserve.

Remember: you are worthy of all the love, joy, and abundance the world has to offer. Your strength is limitless, and you have the ability to create a future filled with peace, empowerment, and self-love. Every step you take on this journey brings you closer to your true self—the person who is confident, strong, and ready to embrace the beauty of life once again.

Take a deep breath, trust yourself, and know that you are not just healing—you are becoming the most powerful, radiant version of yourself.

You have already come so far, and I believe in your ability to keep going, to keep growing, and to step into your true power.
