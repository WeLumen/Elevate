Lesson 5: Devaluation & Constant Criticism

What It Looks Like — A Coach’s Deep Dive

Devaluation and constant criticism can feel like an emotional storm. Recognizing the patterns can help you begin to reclaim your power.

1. Compliments That Turn Into Insults

What begins as praise quickly becomes a jab. Comments seem supportive but carry subtle put-downs, intended to make you feel small.

2. Comparing You to Others to Make You Feel “Less Than”

Statements like "Why can't you be more like..." are not about growth; they're designed to trigger feelings of inadequacy and self-doubt.

3. Making You Feel Like Nothing You Do Is Ever Good Enough

Efforts are invalidated no matter how much you give. The criticism never ends, leaving you exhausted and questioning your value.

4. Nitpicking Every Small Detail to Diminish Your Worth

Persistent fault-finding over minor things builds resentment and insecurity, often projecting the other person's own anxieties onto you.

5. Gaslighting Through Criticism

When your feelings are dismissed with "You're too sensitive," it's emotional manipulation meant to distort your reality.

Holistic Healing Insight — From My Heart to Yours

Your self-worth is not dependent on their approval. Criticism often reflects the critic’s pain, not your shortcomings. Begin healing by affirming your value from within.

Overt Devaluation: The Loud and Obvious

  • "You're so lazy, you never do anything right."
  • "Why are you always messing things up?"
  • "She’s better than you at this."
  • "You'll never amount to anything."

These remarks are meant to dominate and shame.

Covert Devaluation: The Subtle, Stealthy Underminer

  • "Oh, I didn't think you'd actually manage to do that."
  • "You're so sensitive."
  • "That dress is… interesting."
  • "I’m only saying this because I care."
  • "I guess you’re just not cut out for this."

These comments are masked as concern or humor, leaving you doubting your perception.

How These Look in Real Life

  • Workplace
    • Overt: "You're hopeless at this."
    • Covert: "That report is interesting..."
  • Family
    • Overt: "You're disappointing me again."
    • Covert: "You should be more responsible."
  • Romantic Relationships
    • Overt: "You're so messy."
    • Covert: "I guess I'm more organized than you."
  • Friendships
    • Overt: "You're such a drama queen."
    • Covert: "You’re overthinking it."

Real-Life Stories You Might Recognize

The Work Critique: "This isn’t what I expected. Can't you do better?" You gave your best.

Family Remarks: "You've changed." Translation: "You’re not who I want you to be."

Relationship Comparisons: "My ex was more organized." Their words reflect them, not you.

How to Reclaim Your Power

  • Practice Radical Self-Validation: Affirm your feelings and wins. Journal. Be kind to yourself.
  • See the Person, Not Their Words: Their pain speaks, not truth. Don’t take it on.
  • Set Gentle but Firm Boundaries: Decline criticism that doesn’t support your growth.
  • Surround Yourself with Nourishing Relationships: Seek those who uplift and respect you.
  • Turn Inward with Mindfulness and Journaling: Awareness brings transformation and healing.

You are not defined by their judgments. Your worth is rooted in your being, your truth, and your love for yourself. Live free, radiant, and whole.
