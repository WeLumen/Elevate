Lesson 7: Triangulation & Creating Jealousy

What It Really Looks Like in Your Life

Projection and blame-shifting are some of the sneakiest tactics narcissists use to keep you confused and doubting yourself. It’s like they have this toolbox of tricks designed to make you question your own reality and feel like you are the problem — when in fact, they’re the ones doing the emotional damage.

Imagine this: they accuse you of lying, but they’re the ones constantly bending the truth or hiding things. They call you selfish, but they’re the ones who never stop to think about your feelings or respect your boundaries. Or worse, they turn every single conflict around so you feel guilty for how they are acting. Suddenly, their bad behavior isn’t their fault — it’s all on you.

Real-Life Examples of Projection & Blame-Shifting

  • 1. Lying Accusations:

    You find out they’ve been dishonest — maybe they said one thing but you discovered the truth through a text or from someone else. When you try to talk about it, instead of owning up, they turn the tables on you: “You’re the one who’s dishonest. You never tell me the whole story.” Even if you have proof, they refuse to admit it. Instead, they make you feel crazy for doubting them.

  • 2. Cheating Allegations:

    They might accuse you of being unfaithful or emotionally distant — but they’re the ones sneaking texts to someone else. It distracts you from their betrayals and makes you feel ashamed or guilty.

  • 3. Selfishness Claims:

    They say, “You only care about yourself,” while they keep prioritizing their own wants and needs. You’re left feeling like the selfish one while constantly overextending yourself.

  • 4. Responsibility Shifting:

    When you express how their actions hurt you, they respond with: “If you didn’t push me so hard, I wouldn’t be this way.” Your valid emotions are twisted into blame.

Why Narcissists Use Projection & Blame-Shifting

Narcissists use these tactics as defense mechanisms because they can’t face their own flaws. Admitting they’re wrong would destroy their image of perfection. So instead, they project their negative traits onto you.

Blame-shifting is also a form of control — it keeps you off balance and makes you question yourself. It’s a rewriting of reality to maintain power over you.

Healing Insight: Their Projections Are Not Yours to Carry

This is a crucial point for your healing: those accusations and blame games? They belong to them — not to you. Their projections reflect their inner struggles, not your truth.

  • You are not the liar. You’re the one searching for honesty.
  • You are not the cheater. You value loyalty deeply.
  • You are not selfish. You care deeply, sometimes even too much.
  • You are not responsible for their behavior. You cannot control their choices.

How to Start Releasing Their Projections — Step by Step

  • 1. Set a Clear Boundary in Your Mind:

    When their accusations echo in your mind, say: “I will not carry this false blame. It belongs to them.” Visualize yourself releasing that emotional weight.

  • 2. Anchor Yourself in Your Own Truth:

    Write down what really happened and how you felt. When doubt creeps in, return to your notes to stay grounded.

  • 3. Practice Compassionate Detachment:

    Care for the person, but don’t absorb their projections. Picture a loving shield around you that reflects false blame away.

  • 4. Focus on Your Own Healing:

    Redirect your energy inward: meditate, journal, walk in nature, create. Reconnect with your truth and calm.

Remember: Your Power Lies in Not Defending Their Lies

The healing journey isn’t about arguing back — it’s about reclaiming your peace. When you stop defending yourself, you stop giving their projections power over your mind and emotions.

And in that space — where you are free from carrying their shadows — your true self begins to shine. You become whole again, unburdened and radiant.

You’ve got this.
