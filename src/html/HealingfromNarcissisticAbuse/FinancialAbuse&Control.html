Lesson 9: Financial Abuse & Control

What it looks like

When someone you love or trust starts controlling your access to money or financial resources, it’s not just about dollars and cents — it’s about your freedom, your voice, and your ability to make choices for yourself.

You might notice that your partner decides how much money you can spend, where you can shop, or even whether you’re allowed to use your own money at all. They might monitor every purchase or demand receipts for things you buy, making you feel like you have no autonomy.

Sometimes, money becomes a weapon. Maybe when you don’t “behave” or follow their rules, they withhold funds, refuse to pay for essential things like groceries or medical care, or threaten to cut you off financially. This isn’t about care — it’s about control.

There are also subtler ways this plays out:

  • Sabotaging your attempts to build financial independence.
  • Ruining your credit by accumulating debt in your name.
  • Refusing to let you work or pursue education.
  • Blocking your opportunities to learn new skills or earn income.

These actions are designed to keep you feeling powerless and isolated, like you’re constantly tiptoeing around their expectations, walking on eggshells just to avoid another punishment or fight.

If any of this sounds familiar, know that you’re not alone — and this kind of financial abuse is a serious, real form of control that deeply impacts your wellbeing.

Holistic Healing Insight

Money isn’t just numbers on a screen or bills in a wallet — it’s energy, freedom, and sovereignty. When someone else controls your money, they’re trying to control your entire life.

Your financial power belongs to YOU. Reclaiming that power is one of the most profound steps you can take on your healing journey.

Healing begins the moment you acknowledge that you deserve independence, stability, and respect — not just emotionally or spiritually, but in your everyday life and practical reality.

You don’t have to do this all at once. Start small:

  • Open a separate bank account only you control.
  • Seek help from a trusted financial advisor or counselor.
  • Set clear boundaries around your finances.
  • Start an emergency fund, even with just a few dollars weekly.
  • Explore job training programs or online courses to grow your skills and confidence.

Remember: reclaiming your financial freedom is reclaiming your freedom as a woman, as a human being.

You have the right to choose how your money flows, to nurture it, and to use it as a tool for your growth and wellbeing.

You are worthy. You are capable. And this is your sacred journey back to sovereignty — one step, one breath, one empowered choice at a time.
