Lesson 7: Triangulation & Creating Jealousy

Triangulation happens when someone deliberately pulls a third person into your relationship dynamic to unsettle you. This could be a friend, a coworker, or even an ex. Suddenly, you find yourself caught in a game where you’re pitted against someone else—not for fun, but to make you feel insecure and unsure of your place.

They might casually drop comparisons like, “They would never act like you do,” or “They’re so much better at handling things.” You catch yourself wondering, Am I really not good enough? or Do I have to compete for their love and attention?

You might notice them flirting with someone else, or sharing inside jokes with a coworker you don’t know about, leaving you feeling left out and anxious.

A deeper, holistic healing perspective

Triangulation is a manipulation tactic rooted in insecurity and control. It’s designed to trigger your deepest fears—fear of abandonment, fear of not being enough.

You are not in competition with anyone else. Your worth isn’t measured by how you compare to others, and you don’t have to scramble for attention or affection like it’s a prize to be won.

Let’s explore some real-life examples to make this clearer

  • With a partner: They constantly talk about a coworker they admire, making you feel compared or replaced.
  • In friendships: A friend favors a new person and shares secrets with them, leaving you feeling excluded.
  • At work: A colleague is constantly praised while your contributions are ignored, leading to feelings of inadequacy.

Why does triangulation hurt so much?

It taps into our human need to belong and be chosen. This fear of being left out triggers anxiety, self-doubt, and emotional exhaustion.

Healing from Triangulation: Reclaiming Your Peace and Power

By [Your Name], Holistic Relationship & Wellness Coach

  • 1. Recognize the Manipulation: Triangulation is about their insecurities, not your worth. Seeing it clearly gives you power.
  • 2. Remind Yourself: You Are Enough, Exactly as You Are:

    Say daily: “I am whole. I am worthy. I am enough.” This strengthens your sense of self.

  • 3. Set Emotional Boundaries with Love and Firmness:

    Communicate directly: “I want our relationship to be based on honesty and respect. Bringing others into our conflicts isn’t healthy for either of us.”

  • 4. Build Your Inner Security Through Self-Care:
    • Meditation to calm the mind
    • Journaling to understand your emotions
    • Yoga or dancing to energize your body
    • Creative expression to connect with your truth
  • 5. Seek Support When You Need It:

    Healing deep-rooted patterns can be supported by therapy or holistic coaching. You don’t have to do this alone.

A Final, Heart-Centered Truth

Love is not a game of comparison or competition. You deserve to be chosen freely, loved fully, and respected deeply.

You are already enough. Your peace matters. And your healing is sacred.
