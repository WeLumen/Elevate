Lesson 2: Creating your online presence

Welcome back. In this third module, we shift our focus toward operational execution—translating strategy into tangible digital presence.

So far, we’ve covered foundational and strategic aspects of building a digital business. Now, we move into the practical and tactical actions required to bring your e-commerce vision to life. This module outlines essential guidelines to help you take those first decisive steps—from concept to launch—by establishing your company’s online infrastructure.

1. Naming Your Digital Business

One of the earliest—and most strategic—decisions is selecting the name of your business and website. While branding is undoubtedly important, the operational implications of your name shouldn't be overlooked. In many directories or search listings, alphabetical order still matters. Selecting a name that begins with an early letter of the alphabet may give your brand higher visibility, especially in curated lists, industry platforms, and product indexes.

For startups and growing companies, this subtle visibility advantage can translate into real traction. As Guy Kawasaki often points out, emerging brands must leverage every possible edge, no matter how small. Appearing ahead of industry giants—even momentarily—can be the opportunity that sparks recognition.

Additionally, aim for a name with international scalability. Choose terminology that carries positive or neutral associations across global markets. Be mindful of linguistic and cultural implications—many companies have faced challenges when product names unintentionally carried negative meanings in other languages or regions. Examples from the automotive sector—such as the Nissan Moco or Mitsubishi Pajero—underscore the importance of cross-cultural due diligence.

Look for names that are:

  • Easy to pronounce across languages
  • Free from undesirable connotations
  • Memorable and intuitive
  • Potentially symbolic or emotionally resonant (e.g., Amazon, evoking scale and wonder)

2. Securing Your Digital Real Estate

Once the name is defined, the next step is to verify domain name availability. This is your brand’s digital address, and it needs to be secured as early as possible. There are numerous platforms where you can search and register your domain—such as Google Domains, Hover, or Wix. Choose a platform that integrates easily with your existing digital tools (e.g., Google Workspace if you use Gmail professionally).

3. Choosing the Right Domain Extension

Domain extensions are more than just functional—they can support your market positioning. Some common strategies include:

  • Geographic relevance (e.g., .es for Spain, .cl for Chile)
  • Industry-specific relevance (e.g., .edu for educational institutions)
  • Global commercial reach (e.g., .com, which remains the most recognized and trusted extension for international commerce)

You may also notice emerging extensions tailored to specific sectors, lifestyles, or trends. While often more expensive, these can reinforce niche positioning or brand personality.

Pro Tip: To protect your brand from future conflicts or domain squatting, consider securing multiple extensions for your chosen name (e.g., .com, .info, .es, .org). This preemptive action prevents third parties from registering similar domains and ensures full control over your digital identity.

Establishing your e-commerce presence is not just a technical task—it’s a strategic milestone in your commercial journey. As the saying goes, a journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step. Let this be the step that moves your vision from concept to digital reality—strong, visible, and ready for growth.
