Lesson 1: Why can't digital sales wait any longer

Can you imagine running a business today without a computer—or even relying on a typewriter to manage operations? It seems almost inconceivable in the modern world.

Yet, operating a business without a digital sales channel is equivalent to turning your back on the future. It places your strategy out of sync with market realities and exposes your company to significant competitive risks.

Digital commerce is no longer a trend—it’s a fundamental shift in how business is conducted across industries. While in the past some executives viewed e-commerce as a complementary channel, or a "nice-to-have" in B2B markets, the global events of 2020 accelerated a transformation that was already underway. The COVID-19 pandemic drastically altered consumer behavior and purchasing habits, resulting in a surge in online sales growth—often exceeding 20% across sectors.

In this context, the critical question becomes: If your business isn’t selling online, who is capturing your market share?

The strategic importance of digital commerce is undeniable, for several reasons:

1. Sustained Behavioral Shifts

Much of the digital adoption forced by the pandemic has become permanent. Consumers and corporate buyers alike have grown accustomed to digital convenience and expect seamless, omnichannel experiences.

2. Rise of Digital Natives

New generations of both customers and professionals entering the workforce are digital-first by nature. Their buying decisions are increasingly influenced by user experience, speed, and online accessibility—even in complex B2B environments.

3. Global Competition Without Borders

In today’s hyperconnected marketplace, your competitors are no longer limited by geography. A company based in the U.S., Canada, India, or Brazil can capture your clients simply by offering a better digital buying journey. If your business lacks a strong online presence, it may soon lack a presence altogether.

Despite its challenges, digital commerce opens powerful opportunities for growth:

  • Expanded Customer Reach
    An optimized digital channel can significantly increase your customer base and allow access to previously untapped markets.
  • Scalable Revenue Streams
    Online platforms enable fast and scalable sales, often with lower operational costs, especially when compared to traditional sales models.
  • Channel Migration
    As buyer behavior shifts, many sales naturally transition from offline to online. Businesses positioned to capture this shift will consolidate market share.
  • Strategic Defense Against Cannibalization
    Developing a digital channel is not just about growth—it’s also about protecting existing share from digitally savvy competitors.
  • Global Market Access at Low Cost
    Through platforms like Amazon, Mercado Libre, Flipkart, or AliExpress, companies can now enter international markets without the heavy investment required for physical expansion.

However, success in digital sales requires more than just launching a website. Several strategic risks and considerations must be addressed:

  • Organizational Change Management
    Implementing digital sales requires a cultural and operational transformation. Resistance to change is common, and leading teams through this evolution is a crucial leadership skill.
  • Technology & Talent Dependence
    E-commerce platforms evolve rapidly. Businesses must invest in training and continuously upskill teams to meet performance expectations on competitive marketplaces.
  • Assortment Strategy
    Not every product belongs online. Selection must be strategic, based on profitability, logistics capacity, and alignment with online consumer demand. Successful products online often bring hidden costs—logistics, fees, promotions—that must be carefully modeled.

The best approach? Treat your digital channel as a standalone business unit, complete with its own P&L. This allows for more accurate performance analysis, cost control, and scalable growth planning.

When entering digital sales, start lean and strategic. Choose one or two products with steady turnover and healthy margins. Use them to test logistics, understand digital buyer behavior, and refine operations before scaling up. Prematurely listing the entire catalog online can overload operations and erode margins, turning opportunity into risk.

Above all, make space for a progressive rollout. Digital transformation is not a race—it’s a strategic journey. Build the infrastructure, logistics, and customer experience necessary to support it long-term.

So, are digital sales worth it?

Absolutely—when done strategically.
Digital commerce, when managed with intelligence and foresight, becomes a catalyst for profitability, resilience, and long-term competitiveness. It protects your market, extends your brand, and positions your company to thrive in an increasingly digital economy.

Don’t wait for business to decline before acting. The future is already here—and those who embrace it with strategic clarity will lead the next generation of commercial success.
