Module 5: Break Free from People Pleasing

← Back to module overview

Lesson 2 : Body Scanning & Journal Prompts

I’m so glad you’re here

This journey was lovingly created to help you begin reconnecting with your emotions and gently untangling from the patterns of self-abandonment that people-pleasing can create.

This is a sacred invitation to explore how these behaviors might be showing up in your life — and more importantly, how you can begin returning home to yourself.

It was designed with care for women who are ready to release the burden of always being “the good girl” — those of us who have been taught to give and give, often at the cost of our own truth.

I speak to you not from a pedestal, but as someone who deeply understands how exhausting and painful it can feel to constantly seek approval or silence your truth just to keep the peace.

The intention here is to help you gently shed those expectations, soften into your truth, and live a life that is aligned with your own values, your own voice, and your deepest desires.

This is just a glimpse into the deeper healing that is possible — may it bring you clarity, insight, and a sense of coming home to your essence.

Phase One: Becoming Aware

In this first step, we’re going to gently explore the different ways that people-pleasing shows up in your life — not from a place of judgment, but with deep compassion and self-inquiry.

We'll begin by identifying your own patterns and reflecting on how these behaviors may be affecting your emotional well-being, your relationships, and your sense of self.

This exploration will help you start connecting the dots between what you do… and why you do it. That awareness is the first step toward lasting transformation.

What You’ll Need

  • Grab a notebook, journal, or a document on your computer.
  • Something you can write on with freedom and presence.

As you reflect, simply place a gentle checkmark or note beside the statements that resonate — the ones that feel like they reflect a truth in your own life.

Don’t overthink it. Let your intuition guide you. There’s no right or wrong here. This is a space of honest, heart-led reflection.

Gentle Reflection

Now that you’ve completed the list, take a deep breath. Place your hands over your heart if that feels good. This isn’t about scoring yourself or getting anything “right.” It’s simply about becoming more aware of the patterns you’ve been living with, often without realizing.

Take a few moments now to journal:

  • Which of these statements surprised you?
  • Which ones felt especially difficult or painful to acknowledge?
  • Which of these behaviors do you feel have the most impact in your life — on your relationships, your work, your well-being?

Invitation to Deepen

Inside your reflection PDF, you’ll find journaling prompts to help you dive deeper into:

  • Where these behaviors tend to show up (e.g., family, romantic relationships, work)
  • Which people or dynamics feel most triggering or difficult for you
  • The emotional and physical toll of trying to keep everyone happy — resentment, burnout, disconnection from your own desires
  • The gap between who you truly are… and the version of you others see

This is sacred work. Tender, but powerful.

You’ll also explore your WHY — the deeper reason your soul is calling you to make a shift. What kind of woman do you want to become, if you no longer feel the need to shrink, apologize, or overextend?

Let yourself dream a little here. This is where the healing begins.

Take your time with this process. Give yourself grace. And when you’re ready, the next step will be waiting for you with open arms.

Gentle Body Scan: A Loving Journey Through Your Inner Landscape

Find a quiet, cozy space where you can sit or lie down with ease. Allow your body to settle gently. If it feels safe, softly close your eyes and begin to welcome a few slow, nourishing breaths. Inhale deeply... and exhale with a sense of letting go.

Let’s begin this gentle journey inward — a tender exploration of your body, from crown to toes, like a wave of loving presence moving through you.

There’s nothing you need to fix or change here. You’re simply being invited to notice... with curiosity and compassion.

Gently bring your awareness to:

  • The top of your head. How does this space feel right now? Light? Tingling? Still?
  • Your forehead and scalp. Notice if there’s any tightness or ease. Invite softening, if it feels right.
  • Your eyes, cheeks, and jaw. So often we hold our expressions here — can you allow your jaw to soften just a little?
  • Your neck and shoulders. Breathe into this space. Let it be just as it is. Feel any heaviness or sensation without resistance.
  • Your arms, elbows, and hands. Feel their weight. Notice the temperature of your skin or the textures they rest against.
  • Your chest and upper back. Tune into your breath, the gentle rise and fall. No need to control — just observe.
  • Your belly and lower back. Allow your breath to travel here, offering spaciousness and care.
  • Your hips, pelvis, and glutes. What sensations are present here? Is this area asking for softness or support?
  • Your thighs, knees, and legs. Feel the connection with the earth or the surface beneath you — a steady, grounding presence.
  • And finally, your feet and toes. Notice where they meet the ground. Feel the quiet support of being held.

Pause here. Take a few slower, deeper breaths. Gently sense your whole body now, as one interconnected being. How do you feel?

This body scan isn’t just about awareness — it’s an act of reverence. It reminds you that your body is your companion, your home, your guide. With regular practice, this gentle ritual can deepen your relationship with yourself, helping you reconnect, regulate, and restore.

If you discovered tension during this scan, here's an invitation to reflect with tenderness:

  • Breathe into that place. Let your awareness gently rest there. No need to change anything — just notice what’s true.
  • Hold it with loving curiosity. What’s the shape of this tension? Does it shift? Is it sharp, dull, heavy, warm?
  • Feel beneath the surface. Might this tension be speaking an emotion? Perhaps stress, sadness, anticipation, or something unnamed?
  • Use your breath as medicine. Imagine each inhale bringing light, and each exhale melting tension away — or simply offering space.
  • Bring mindfulness into your day. Does this area of tension show up at certain times or around certain people or situations? What wisdom might it hold for you?

Remember: your body speaks in whispers. When you slow down to listen, you begin to understand its language. You’re not here to force anything into ease — you’re here to meet yourself with grace.

You are already whole. And your body — even with its aches and stories — is not a problem to solve, but a sacred part of your journey.

Soulful Journal Prompts for Coming Home to Yourself

Journaling is a sacred space — a quiet, intimate moment where you can meet yourself just as you are. Let this be a gentle invitation to slow down, breathe deeply, and turn inward with kindness. There’s no need to rush or get it "right." Simply allow your heart to guide the way.

These prompts are here to hold space for your truth, your healing, and your rediscovery of what it means to honor yourself fully. Let them be flexible companions — adapt them however you need. And above all, approach each word with love, softness, and a willingness to feel without judgment.

Take your time with each one. Light a candle. Make tea. Let it feel sacred.

Reflection Prompts

  • How do you tend to respond when someone asks you for help or a favor?
    Pause and notice what thoughts, emotions, or patterns arise in those moments. Is there a part of you that wants to say no but feels afraid to disappoint?
  • What are the signs your body gives you when you’ve been giving too much?
    Do you notice tightness in your chest, a knot in your stomach, fatigue, or restlessness? Your body often speaks before your mind catches up.
  • Recall a recent moment when you felt torn between pleasing someone and honoring your own boundaries.
    How did that moment feel in your body? What emotions surfaced? Were you able to stay true to yourself?
  • Reflect on a memory from childhood that may have shaped your need to please others.
    Was love ever conditional? Were you praised for being “good” or agreeable? What beliefs might have formed in those early years?
  • What emotions arise when you think about saying “no” or setting a boundary?
    Is there guilt? Fear of rejection? A desire to be liked? Imagine what it would feel like to choose your well-being without apology.
  • Think of a time when you silenced your own feelings to keep the peace or avoid conflict.
    What did it cost you in the short term? And in the long term? How would it feel to give that part of yourself a voice now?
  • Is there someone whose validation you keep seeking?
    Explore why their opinion holds weight. What would shift for you if you no longer needed their approval to feel worthy?
  • Write about a recent moment when you compromised your truth to make someone else comfortable.
    How did that sit in your body and your heart? What might your inner self have needed instead?
  • Name a hobby, activity, or passion that lights you up — something that feels like home to your soul.
    Have you been making space for it? Or have others’ needs taken priority? How can you begin to reclaim that joy?
  • Imagine a day where you live completely aligned with your truth — no guilt, no fear, no apology.
    What does that day look like? How do you feel in your body, your heart, your spirit? What shifts when you choose yourself with love?

Dear one, this journey of reflection is not about fixing or changing who you are — it’s about remembering.

Remembering your worth. Remembering your needs matter. Remembering that your voice, your feelings, and your truth deserve to be honored — always.

The road ahead is full of possibility, and it begins right here, with you.
