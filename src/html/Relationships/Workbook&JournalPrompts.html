Module 6: Your Guide To Assertive Communication

Workbook & Journal Prompts: Communication Styles — A Holistic Perspective

The way we communicate is a reflection of how we relate to ourselves and others. Each of us has a natural communication style — a pattern shaped by our life experiences, emotional safety, and internal beliefs. Gently becoming aware of our unique style helps us understand not only how we express our thoughts and needs, but also how we hold space for others in connection.

When we bring loving awareness to our communication habits, we begin to heal patterns that no longer serve us. We open the door to more authentic conversations, deeper trust, and nourishing relationships — with others and with ourselves.

Understanding the Main Communication Styles

  • Assertive: You speak your truth with clarity and respect, honoring both your needs and the needs of others.
    “I appreciate the ideas in this proposal, and I also have some concerns about the financial projections. Maybe we could revisit the numbers together?”
  • Passive: You tend to hold back your thoughts or preferences to avoid discomfort, sometimes leaving your own needs unmet.
    “I’m not really sure… whatever you think is best.”
  • Aggressive: You express yourself in a forceful or dominating way, which can overwhelm or silence others.
    “This proposal is a waste of time. Let’s just move on.”
  • Passive-Aggressive: You might express your frustrations in indirect ways, often masking true feelings behind politeness or sarcasm.
    “I guess we can try this idea… even though it might be a waste of time.”
  • Collaborative: You aim for harmony and mutual understanding, creating space for everyone’s voice to be heard.

Overview: How Each Style Affects Connection

  • Assertive: “I feel that...” “I understand your view, and...”
    Fosters trust, clarity, and mutual respect.
    Be mindful of: Can be mistaken for harshness if empathy isn’t present.
  • Aggressive: “You never...” “It’s your fault...”
    Can create fear, conflict, and defensiveness.
    Be mindful of: Take time to ground before speaking. Reflect before reacting.
  • Passive: “It’s okay, whatever you want.”
    Leads to feeling unseen or unheard over time.
    Be mindful of: Remember your voice matters. Practice gentle self-expression.
  • Passive-Aggressive: “Fine, do what you want.”
    Causes confusion and disconnection.
    Be mindful of: Tune into your real feelings. Name them with care.
  • Submissive: “I just want to keep the peace.”
    Often leads to self-neglect.
    Be mindful of: You’re allowed to take up space and share your truth.
  • Manipulative: “If you loved me, you’d do this.”
    Undermines trust and creates imbalance.
    Be mindful of: Notice when fear or insecurity wants to control others.
  • Competitive: “I can do it better.”
    Can block collaboration and connection.
    Be mindful of: There’s beauty in co-creation. Honor everyone’s gifts.
  • Avoidant: Silence, changing the subject, leaving the room.
    Avoids resolution, leaving wounds unhealed.
    Be mindful of: Practice staying present. You are safe to be seen and heard.

Reflect & Refine — A Heart-Centered Practice

Take a gentle moment to tune in and reflect:

  • What communication style(s) do you most resonate with right now?
  • How has this way of relating shaped your experiences in relationships, both personally and professionally?
  • Where do you feel called to grow or soften? How would a more balanced, loving expression feel?

Quiz: Discover Your Communication Style

This self-inquiry is here to support awareness, not judgment. Let your answers be honest and kind — this is a space of growth, not perfection.

Scoring Guide

  • Mostly A’s: You may lean toward a Passive style. Your voice matters, and it’s safe to take up space.
  • Mostly B’s: You might express yourself through an Aggressive lens. You are strong — and your softness is just as powerful.
  • Mostly C’s: You may show Passive-Aggressive tendencies. Honesty, spoken with care, can liberate and heal.
  • Mostly D’s: You communicate with Assertiveness. A beautiful balance of clarity and compassion — keep nurturing it.

Final Reflection

This isn’t about being “right” or “wrong” — it’s about being real. Wherever you are in your communication journey, meet yourself with gentleness. With awareness comes the power to shift, to soften, to grow. And every step you take toward clearer, kinder expression brings more truth, harmony, and connection into your world.

Journaling Prompts for Conscious Communication

These journaling reflections are here to support you in deepening your connection with your inner voice and cultivating more authentic, empowered communication. As you explore each question, breathe gently and meet yourself with kindness. There is no “right” answer — only your truth, in this moment.

Let your heart lead the way.

Reflecting on Your Communication Journey

  • Think of a recent moment where you felt truly heard and expressed.
    What did you do that supported clear, authentic communication? How did it feel in your body and heart?
  • Now, gently recall a time when communication felt difficult or unclear.
    What do you sense may have been holding you back — fear, self-doubt, old patterns? Be compassionate with yourself as you explore this.
  • How would you describe your current communication style?
    Is it soft and accommodating, bold and direct, subtle and indirect, or a mixture? How do you feel about the way you tend to express yourself?
  • Take a moment to write down some words or phrases you often use when speaking with others.
    Do these words reflect the way you wish to show up in your relationships — with clarity, kindness, and presence?
  • Reflect on your early experiences with communication.
    How did the environment you grew up in shape the way you express your needs, feelings, and boundaries today?
  • Are there certain situations, people, or emotions that tend to trigger you into a less conscious or less empowered style of communication?
    What do you notice about these moments? What might your inner self be needing?
  • Imagine your life if you communicated with more confidence, calm, and clarity.
    How would your relationships shift? How would it feel to speak from your truth without fear or apology?
  • Think of someone you admire as a communicator.
    What is it about their energy, presence, or words that inspires you? How do they make others feel safe and respected?
  • What inner or outer barriers might you face as you move toward a more balanced, assertive communication style?
    How can you meet those edges with grace, patience, and self-compassion?
  • Let’s ground this growth into action.
    Choose one gentle step you can take this week to embody more empowered communication. It could be speaking up in a small moment, pausing to breathe before responding, or expressing a need with love.
    What will you practice? How will you support yourself in the process?

Remember: communication is not just about the words we say — it’s about the energy we carry, the presence we offer, and the love we allow ourselves to receive and give. Keep meeting yourself where you are. You're doing beautifully.
