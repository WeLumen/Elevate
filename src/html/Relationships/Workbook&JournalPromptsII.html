Module 6: Your Guide To Assertive Communication

← Back to module overview

Workbook & Journal Prompts: BOUNDARY MAPPING: A Gentle Journey into Your Inner Landscape

Welcome, beautiful soul. This is your sacred space to explore where your energy feels most at home, and where it feels stretched or compromised. In this journey, we will lovingly map the areas of your life that are asking for a little more clarity, care, and conscious attention through the setting of gentle, empowering boundaries.

Step 1: Identifying Your Life Areas

Begin by bringing awareness to the main areas of your life. Some examples may include:

  • Work & Career
  • Relationships (romantic, family, friendships)
  • Personal Time & Self-Care
  • Emotional Wellbeing
  • Physical & Energetic Space

Take a few moments to list the areas that feel most relevant to your life right now. There is no rush—let your heart guide you.

Step 2: Gently Naming Your Boundaries

For each area you identified, ask yourself:

  • Where do I feel my energy being drained?
  • Where do I feel resentful, overwhelmed, or unacknowledged?
  • What would feel more nourishing or supportive?

Now, softly write down the boundaries that would help protect and restore your energy. These might sound like:

  • "I give myself permission to not respond to work emails after 6 p.m."
  • "I will reserve one night a week just for me—no plans, no screens."
  • "I gently say no to unsolicited advice from loved ones."

Next to each boundary, rate how important it feels on a scale from 1 to 10. This isn’t to judge, but to bring gentle clarity to what needs your attention most.

Step 3: Create Your Boundary Map

Imagine this as your energetic map—your personal guide to where your yes lives, and where your sacred no begins. Keep this map close, and revisit it often. It will evolve as you do.

Use the space below to draw, write, or mind-map your boundaries. You can use colors, shapes, or symbols if that feels supportive. This is a space of creative reflection.

Step 4: How Will You Share Your Boundaries?

It can be tender to express our boundaries, especially if we fear disappointing others. But remember, communicating your needs is an act of love—for yourself and for those around you.

Reflect on how you might communicate these boundaries with care and clarity:

  • Will you speak in person, write a message, or gently assert your needs in the moment?
  • What words feel both kind and firm?

Here are some gentle NLP-inspired language patterns to support you:

  • Presupposition: "Once we begin honoring each other's time, I believe we’ll feel more connected."
  • Embedded Command: "I appreciate it when people give me space to reflect before making decisions."
  • Softeners: "I wonder if we might try..."
  • Double Binds: "Would it feel better for you if I shared this now or later this evening?"

Practice crafting a few sentences that feel true to your voice. Speak them aloud. Notice how they feel in your body.

Step 5: Reframing Boundaries as Self-Love

Before moving forward, take a breath and reframe any guilt or resistance. Boundaries are not walls; they are bridges to deeper connection, built on the foundation of mutual respect.

Instead of seeing them as “limitations,” hold them as loving invitations: invitations to be more you, to live with integrity, and to feel safe in your own skin.

Repeat to yourself: "My boundaries are acts of self-love. They create space for my joy, peace, and truth to blossom."

Step 6: Your Gentle Timeline

You don’t have to set all your boundaries at once. Choose 1 or 2 to begin with. Give yourself a loving timeline.

What will you implement this week? What can wait until next month?

Write down your initial action steps:

  • Which boundaries will I begin with?
  • When will I communicate them?
  • What support do I need as I do this?

Remember, your timeline is yours. Let it flow with grace.

Step 7: Practice and Accountability

Practicing new boundaries is a brave act. Be patient with yourself. Slip-ups are part of the process—they are invitations to come back to yourself with compassion.

You might choose to:

  • Role-play with a trusted friend or coach
  • Journal about your experiences
  • Revisit your scripts and update them
  • Celebrate small wins along the way

If it feels right, share your boundary intentions with someone who will lovingly support you. Accountability can be a sacred form of encouragement.

Anchoring Empowerment: A Mini Ritual

Recall a moment when you felt truly empowered in setting a boundary. Close your eyes and let yourself relive it:

  • What did you say?
  • How did your body feel?
  • What did you see, hear, sense?

Place your hand on your heart or press your thumb and finger together—whatever gesture feels intuitive. Breathe in the feeling. This is your anchor. You can return to it anytime you need strength.

Journaling Prompts & Gentle Affirmations

Empowered Voice: Nurturing Assertive Communication

Affirmations for Heart-Centered Communication

Take a moment to connect with your breath and gently read each affirmation out loud or in your heart. Let these words sink into your body like soft roots grounding you in your truth.

  • I honor my inner wisdom and give myself full permission to set clear and loving boundaries.
  • My voice carries value, beauty, and truth — and it is safe to be heard.
  • With each conversation, I deepen my capacity to listen with presence and empathy.
  • I trust my ability to build heartfelt connections with others.
  • I express my needs with grace using "I" statements that reflect who I truly am.
  • I am worthy of creating boundaries that protect and replenish my energy.
  • I welcome meaningful conversations that nourish my soul.
  • I respect the boundaries of others and invite mutual respect in return.
  • I feel empowered in my assertiveness — it anchors me in my authenticity.
  • I use gentle and effective NLP tools to create positive, conscious shifts in dialogue.
  • I embrace active listening as a sacred practice that brings me closer to those I love.
  • Every day, my communication flows with more clarity, kindness, and confidence.
  • I navigate challenges with calm, grounded presence and thoughtful expression.
  • I bring compassion and patience into every interaction, including with myself.
  • My assertiveness is a beautiful reflection of my self-worth and inner integrity.

Reflective Journaling Prompts

Let these gentle invitations guide your inner exploration. Approach them with honesty, curiosity, and tenderness — there's no right or wrong here, just the unfolding of your unique voice.

  • In which situations do I feel called to be more assertive? What makes these moments challenging or meaningful for me?
  • How do I feel—emotionally and physically—when I successfully set a boundary? What do I notice when I avoid doing so?
  • What are three compassionate phrases I can use to enhance my active listening presence?
  • Reflect on a recent moment where I could have used "I" statements. How might the energy of that conversation have shifted?
  • In what ways can I weave NLP-inspired language into my daily communication, gently and naturally?
  • Which communication or coaching tools feel most aligned with my essence as I grow in assertiveness?
  • Describe a past situation where using the S.T.A.R. technique (Situation – Task – Action – Result) might have supported a clearer outcome.
  • What inner or outer blocks arise when I try to stay fully present during conversations? How can I soften into those moments?
  • How do I imagine my relationships and sense of self evolving as I embody more empowered communication?
  • Write a heartfelt letter to your future self — the version of you who communicates clearly, lovingly, and with deep confidence. What do you want her to know, remember, and celebrate?
