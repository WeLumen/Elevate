Module 6: Your Guide To Assertive Communication

← Back to module overview

Workbook & Journal Prompts: Body Language for Assertiveness — Gentle Self-Awareness Workbook

Dear one,

The way we carry ourselves speaks volumes—even before we utter a word. This tender guide is here to help you deepen your connection to your body and embody the calm, grounded presence that naturally radiates confidence and respect. Use this checklist not as a list of “shoulds,” but as a loving reflection of where you’re thriving and where gentle shifts could support your growth.

Body Language Self-Reflection Checklist

Take a slow breath, soften your shoulders, and gently review each area below with curiosity and compassion:

  • Eye Contact with Presence: Meeting someone’s gaze (even just 30–70% of the time) can express warmth, sincerity, and a sense of self-trust. Let it be soft, not forced—an exchange of energy, not dominance.
  • Nodding with Intention: A subtle, occasional nod can express your presence and attunement. Be mindful not to over-nod, which can sometimes signal insecurity or a need for approval.
  • Upright, Open Posture: Standing or sitting tall with relaxed shoulders helps you feel more grounded and lets others feel your presence. It says, “I am here, and I honor myself.”
  • Gentle Mirroring: Softly reflecting someone’s body language can foster connection. Let it happen organically, as a quiet way to say, “I see you.”
  • Open Gestures: Hands visible, gestures spacious—this openness invites trust and signals that you’re approachable and clear.
  • Embody Your Power: Before a big conversation, try a few empowering stances—feet planted, heart lifted. These simple poses can reconnect you with your inner strength.
  • Purposeful Hand Movements: Let your hands dance with your words—but gently. Controlled, calm gestures amplify clarity; exaggerated ones can dilute your message.
  • Smiling with Authenticity: A real smile can brighten the moment, but there's no need to smile through discomfort. Your authenticity is more powerful than performance.
  • Voice Like a River: Speak with a steady, kind tone. Use pauses. Let your pitch and pace match your grounded presence. Downward inflections help your words land with certainty.
  • Stillness as Strength: Fidgeting often comes from anxiety. When we pause and stay present in our bodies, our message becomes clearer, our energy more rooted.

Nonverbal Communication: Do’s & Don’ts

Do:

  • Let your facial expressions reflect your truth.
  • Make warm, appropriate eye contact.
  • Maintain an upright, open posture—arms uncrossed, shoulders relaxed.
  • Use gentle gestures to support your words.
  • Respect personal space—yours and theirs.
  • Use touch only when it’s welcome and appropriate.
  • Let your voice be calm, clear, and aligned with your intention.

Don’t:

  • Force expressions that don’t match your inner state.
  • Stare or use intense eye contact to dominate.
  • Slouch or close off your body—it may suggest fear or disinterest.
  • Use aggressive gestures or point fingers.
  • Violate someone’s physical or energetic space.
  • Touch without permission—always honor boundaries.
  • Shout or whisper—find your balanced tone.

Embodied Practice Tips

  • Mirror Wisdom: Practice in front of a mirror. Let your gestures feel natural. Smile, breathe, speak. How does your body feel when aligned with your truth?
  • Record with Love: Watch a video of yourself gently. Notice what feels congruent, what feels like it wants more softness or strength.
  • Ask a Trusted Soul: Sometimes, another's loving eyes can reflect what we miss. Invite feedback from someone who holds your heart well.
  • Practice in Real Life: Every interaction is a rehearsal for becoming more you. Try a small tweak each day—a posture, a tone, a pause.

Sacred Steps to Setting Boundaries

  • Self-Awareness: Notice where you feel drained, resentful, or overextended. These are invitations to set loving limits.
  • Define Your Boundaries: Ask yourself gently, “What do I need to feel safe, respected, and free?”
  • Prioritize What Matters: Some things are non-negotiable. Honor them. Your peace is precious.
  • Speak with Grace and Strength: “I value our relationship, and I also need to protect my time. I won’t be available after 5:30 anymore.” Say it with warmth and conviction.
  • Choose a Safe Space to Speak: Find a calm, private setting to share your truth—no rush, no pressure.
  • Hold the Line with Compassion: When others test your boundary, gently remind them. You don’t owe long explanations—just consistency and kindness.
  • Reflect and Adjust: Life evolves. So can your boundaries. Revisit what’s working and what needs refining.
  • Celebrate Small Wins: Did you say no when it mattered? That’s huge. Celebrate it. Let joy be part of your growth.
  • Accountability: Share your boundaries with a soul you trust. Invite loving support.

Action Plan for Real-Life Embodiment

  • Self-Reflection: Where do I most need boundaries or stronger voice?
  • Prioritize: Start where the need feels greatest.
  • Craft Your Words: Choose phrases that feel empowered and kind.
  • Test in Gentle Spaces: Try your new voice in low-stakes settings first.
  • Reflect After Each Try: What felt good? What needs softening or strengthening?
  • Refine Your Body Language: Embody your message—your posture, tone, and energy matter.
  • Keep Growing: Stay curious. Your voice is a lifelong dance.

Emotional Intelligence Tools

  • Pause & Breathe: The sacred pause brings you back to yourself. Before reacting, take three deep breaths. Feel your feet. Name what you're feeling.
  • Active Listening: Listen not just to words, but to energy. What’s underneath? Can you respond from empathy, not urgency?
  • Name It to Tame It: Naming an emotion—“I feel anxious,” “I feel hurt”—helps to soften its grip.
  • Mirror with Compassion: Subtly reflecting someone's posture or energy can soothe nervous systems and foster trust.
  • Rehearse with Presence: Visualize the interaction. See yourself staying calm, centered, and kind. Your nervous system learns from imagination.
  • S.T.O.P. Practice: Stop. Take a breath. Observe. Proceed. This practice can be your anchor in hard moments.

Emotional Self-Check

  • Did I pause and breathe today before reacting?
  • Did I accurately name what I was feeling?
  • Did I truly listen—with my heart as well as my ears?
  • Was my inner dialogue kind and supportive?
  • Did I note any emotional patterns or triggers?
  • Did I stay connected to myself while engaging with others?

Emotional Intelligence Quiz

This is not a test. It’s a mirror. Be honest, be gentle.

Reflection Worksheets

  • Emotion Thermometer: What emotion did I feel today? How strong was it (1–10)? What helped soothe it?
  • Emotion Detective: What triggered my emotion? What was the deeper story beneath it? What does this emotion want me to know?
  • Self-Regulation Reflection: What challenge tested my regulation today? What strategies helped—or could help—me stay centered?

Journaling Prompts & Soulful Affirmations

~ Embracing Assertiveness with Grace & Self-Compassion ~

Dear one,

This is a sacred space for you to explore your truth, meet your emotions with tenderness, and lovingly strengthen your boundaries. Journaling is not about getting it “right”—it’s about getting real. Let your pen flow with honesty and heart. No judgment, just gentle curiosity.

Soul Affirmations for Empowered Communication

Let these affirmations gently anchor you as you reclaim your voice and honor your worth:

  • I give myself permission to express my needs and desires clearly, kindly, and with confidence.
  • My boundaries are sacred reflections of my self-worth, and I tend to them with love and intention.
  • I deserve to be treated with respect, dignity, and tenderness—always.
  • Saying “no” when it honors my truth is an act of self-love, not rejection.
  • Assertiveness is a gentle ally that helps me live a more balanced and authentic life.
  • I express my boundaries with clarity and compassion—without guilt or apology.
  • My time is valuable, and I protect it with wise and heart-centered boundaries.
  • Being assertive deepens my relationships and empowers me to be fully myself.
  • I allow my boundaries to evolve as I grow, honoring what I need in each new season.
  • I trust myself to stand firm in my decisions and communicate them with grace.
  • It’s safe to prioritize my needs—it isn’t selfish, it’s sacred self-care.
  • I honor and respect the boundaries of others and invite the same in return.
  • I am creating a safe and nurturing emotional space by lovingly protecting my boundaries.
  • I can receive support, but my boundaries are mine to define and uphold.
  • Every time I speak my truth with kindness, I strengthen the foundation of my self-respect.

Heart-Led Journaling Prompts

Pour your truth onto the page with compassion. Let this be your space to connect, release, and realign.

  • Are there any boundaries you wish you had honored or set earlier in life? What held you back, and what have you learned since then?
  • How do you feel in moments when you need to be assertive—empowered, hesitant, or something else entirely?
  • Recall a time when a lack of boundaries led to stress or discomfort. If you could rewrite that moment, what would you change?
  • Describe a recent moment when you spoke up for yourself with clarity and calm. How did it feel in your body? What did it shift for you?
  • What boundaries feel absolutely non-negotiable in your personal and professional life right now? Why are they important to you?
  • How do you typically respond when someone crosses a boundary? What’s one more nurturing or empowered way you could handle it?
  • What emotional intelligence skills would you like to cultivate to better support your boundary setting and self-regulation?
  • Reflect on your recent interactions. Are you attuned to the emotional cues of others? How do you respond when you sense discomfort or resistance?
  • Which emotions feel most difficult for you to navigate? Where do you feel them in your body, and what might they be trying to tell you?
  • Describe a moment where you managed your emotions with wisdom and grace. What tools or inner resources supported you?
