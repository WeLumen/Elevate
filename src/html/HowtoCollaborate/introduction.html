Introduction

Welcome to your course How to Collaborate & Not Compete with other Women in the same industry. This course is divided into 5 modules.

Module 1: Historical and Cultural Foundations

This module explores the historical and cultural forces that shaped women’s relationships with one another. It dives into how patriarchal systems and societal structures broke the bonds of sisterhood and offers pathways for rebuilding trust and solidarity through awareness, collective strength, and reconnection to ancestral legacies.

Module 2: Psychological and Evolutionary Perspectives

This module examines the psychological and evolutionary factors that influence women’s dynamics. It highlights the impact of social comparison on self-worth, unveiling how these inner reflections can either trigger competition or serve as mirrors for self-growth and empowerment.

Module 3: Sociological and Structural Factors

This module focuses on how cultural narratives, stereotypes, and media representations shape women’s perceptions of each other. It challenges harmful myths, like the “catfight” trope, and encourages the rewriting of stories that celebrate cooperation and collective achievement.

Module 4: Intersectional Considerations – Embracing the Full Spectrum of Womanhood

This module brings an intersectional lens to the discussion, emphasizing how race, class, culture, and other identities influence women’s lived experiences. It explores the complexity of womanhood, the overlapping layers of oppression, and how embracing these realities fosters a stronger, more inclusive sisterhood.

Module 5: How to Shift From Competition to Collaboration

The final module provides practical tools and frameworks to replace rivalry with collaboration. It covers emotional intelligence, building supportive spaces, rewriting personal and collective narratives, challenging toxic tropes, fostering collective healing, and reclaiming ancestral rituals and community connections as a source of empowerment and unity.
