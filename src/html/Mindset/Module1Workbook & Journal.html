Lesson 2: Module 1 Workbook & Journal

Beloved, welcome to a sacred space of self-reflection. This workbook is not about fixing or judging—it’s about gently uncovering the stories you’ve carried for years, stories that may have shaped how you see yourself and the world around you.

Together, we’ll begin to compassionately explore the origins of your core beliefs and gently question which ones are still serving the radiant woman you are becoming.

Part One: Honoring the Origin of Your Beliefs

Take a few deep, nourishing breaths. Place a hand over your heart, and when you feel ready, softly begin…

  • Name the Belief: List 5 to 7 beliefs you hold about yourself, your relationships, your purpose, your work, and life in general.
  • Where Did This Come From? Reflect on the possible roots of each belief.
  • How Old Was I? Gently recall how old you might have been when this belief was first formed.
  • Moments of Reinforcement: Can you recall specific moments when this belief was echoed or strengthened?
  • Is This Belief Lifting Me or Limiting Me? Label each belief as either empowering or limiting.
  • What Do I Feel When I Think This? Tune into the emotional energy each belief holds.
  • Is This Still True for Me Now? Ask your wise, present-day self if this belief still supports your life today.

Pause here for reflection. What patterns do you notice? What themes are ready to be healed?

Cognitive Distortions: Shifting the Lens

Sometimes the mind tells stories that are louder than truth. Here are some common thought distortions, gently translated into awareness:

  • All-or-Nothing Thinking: Try softening the edges—what’s in the middle space?
  • “Shoulds”: Replace “should” with “could” or “choose”.
  • Discounting the Positive: You do matter. Let your wins count.
  • Overgeneralization: One moment doesn’t define the whole.
  • Labeling: You are not your mistakes.
  • Blame & Personalization: Release what was never yours to carry.
  • Catastrophizing: Not every storm becomes a hurricane.
  • Mind Reading: Make space for curiosity.
  • Emotional Reasoning: Emotions are real—but not always truth.
  • Minimizing: Your feelings are valid.
  • Fortune Telling: The future is unwritten.
  • Mental Filter: Let your vision widen.

As you notice these patterns, gently ask: “What’s a softer, kinder way to see this?”

Journaling Prompts for Gentle Inner Work

  • What is a belief that has shaped your life philosophy?
  • Name three beliefs that uplift and strengthen you.
  • Recall a moment that made you question a belief you once held as truth.
  • How do your beliefs quietly influence your daily routines?
  • What do you believe about joy and what it takes to feel it?
  • What do you believe about your body and its wisdom?
  • Is there a cultural or societal belief you’ve outgrown or embraced? Why?
  • If you could whisper one of your core beliefs to the world, what would it be—and why does it matter?

Final Loving Note:
This journey isn’t about erasing who you’ve been—it’s about reclaiming who you truly are beneath the layers. May this workbook be a sacred mirror, gently guiding you home to your truth, your worth, your light.
