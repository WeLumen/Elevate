The Cognitive Triangle: A Sacred Mirror for Inner Awareness

Imagine a soft triangle glowing gently at the center of your being — three sacred points held in balance: your thoughts, your feelings, and your actions. This is the Cognitive Triangle — a tender framework from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) that invites us into compassionate self-inquiry and soulful healing.

In the holistic healing journey, we come to understand that every reaction, every choice, every emotion — even the ones we wish away — is simply a whisper from the self, asking to be seen with love.

The Triangle Speaks:

  • Situation: A moment, memory, or trigger that stirs something in you.
  • Thoughts: The inner narratives, conscious or unconscious, that arise.
  • Feelings: The emotions your body holds and reflects.
  • Behaviors: The ways you respond — through action or avoidance.

When we shift one point, the whole system begins to soften, recalibrate, and realign with our highest truth. There is no pressure to fix it all — only to bring light to one tender corner at a time.

Soul Reflection: Mapping Your Triangle

Take a sacred pause. Let your breath flow like a stream. Place your hand on your heart if it feels soothing. Then gently journal your reflections below:

  • What recent moment or situation has activated something within you? (A conversation, rejection, a missed opportunity, a conflict, or something left undone.)
  • What inner dialogue unfolded as you sat with this moment? (Let the thoughts come freely. Even the raw ones. Let them be witnessed.)
  • What emotions stirred in your body and spirit as these thoughts took root? (Were you sad, ashamed, anxious, numb, overwhelmed?)
  • How did these feelings influence your actions or choices? (Did you withdraw, procrastinate, lash out, overcompensate, numb, or avoid?)

Self-Sabotage: A Wound That Wants to Be Loved, Not Judged

We often think of self-sabotage as failure. But darling, it’s not. Self-sabotage is your nervous system saying, “I don’t feel safe yet to expand.” It is a part of you — younger, frightened, protective — trying to keep you safe by holding you back.

But what once protected you may now be limiting you. This module is your invitation to gently unhook from that pattern and come home to your radiant potential.

The Sacred Pathway to Healing Self-Sabotage

Step 1: Choose the Area Your Soul Longs to Heal. Is it your career, relationships, self-expression, creative voice, abundance, or body? Trust what calls to you.

“I often hold myself back when it comes to…” (complete the sentence)

Step 2: Name the Ways You Sabotage with Compassion. What does your self-sabotage look like here? Be lovingly honest. Common forms include: procrastination, perfectionism, people-pleasing, avoidance, harsh self-criticism, quitting before finishing, shrinking from visibility.

“When I feel vulnerable in this area, I tend to…”

Step 3: Recall a Recent Moment. Describe a time when this pattern surfaced. What happened? What did you do or not do? Invite the moment in — not as evidence of failure, but as a gateway to healing.

Step 4: Find the Triggers Beneath the Surface. Was it fear of failure? Fear of being judged? Of being seen? Of being too much — or not enough?

“This pattern usually gets activated when I…”

Step 5: Witness the Thought Loop. What story do you tell yourself when you fall into this pattern? It might sound like:

  • “I’m going to mess this up.”
  • “I’ll be rejected if I try.”
  • “I don’t know enough.”
  • “It’s safer to stay invisible.”

Write down 2–3 core beliefs you carry here. Now ask gently: Are these really your truths, or were they inherited, conditioned, or wounded into you?

Step 6: Breathe Into the Emotions. What emotions lie beneath this pattern? Name them softly. Shame, fear, sadness, grief, inadequacy, loneliness — all are welcome. All deserve tending.

Step 7: Trace the Root Wound. Is there a younger version of you who first felt this? Maybe a childhood moment of rejection, comparison, failure, or invisibility? Let her know she is safe now. You are here with her. You are re-parenting the wound with grace.

Sacred Reframe: Choosing New, Nourishing Beliefs

Let’s plant seeds of self-love and truth:

  • Instead of “I’m going to mess this up,” I choose to believe: “I can trust myself through the learning.”
  • Instead of “I’m not enough,” I choose: “I am whole as I am. My worth is not up for debate.”

Write down 3 new soul-anchored truths you are ready to affirm. Make them gentle. Loving. Rooted in the truth of who you are becoming.

The Pattern Interrupt: A Portal to New Possibility

This sacred tool from NLP allows you to pause and choose differently — in real time.

How to Practice:

  • Name the old pattern (e.g., “I spiral into self-doubt when receiving feedback.”)
  • Pick a sacred interrupt. Try:
    • Tap your heart while whispering, “I choose peace.”
    • Light a candle.
    • Place one hand on your womb and one on your heart.
    • Take three long exhales and say, “I am safe.”
  • When the trigger arises, pause. Interrupt it. Breathe. Ask: “What does the empowered version of me choose right now?”
  • Respond with love. Even the smallest shift is holy.

Your Loving Integration Plan

Create a soft action plan to anchor this healing:

  • What small, doable step can I take this week that affirms my worth?
  • How can I honor my progress daily?
  • What support system or resource (coach, friend, journal, ritual) can hold me as I grow?

Closing Affirmations:

Whisper them to yourself. Breathe them in. Feel them take root.

  • “I trust the timing of my becoming.”
  • “My self-sabotage is not my identity — it’s a wound I am healing with love.”
  • “I am safe to take up space.”
  • “I release perfection and embrace my sacred unfolding.”
  • “Every small step I take is a declaration of my worth.”

Beloved one, this work is deep — and it is divine. Self-sabotage does not mean you are broken. It means a part of you once learned to survive through withdrawal, hesitation, or self-protection. Now, you are re-teaching yourself how to thrive.

Take your time. Rest often. Let your healing be soft. Let it be sacred.
