Lesson 1: Define Your Brand Voice

Your brand voice is your personality in words. It’s not just what you say — it’s how you say it. It’s the tone, rhythm, and emotion behind your message. It’s the attitude your audience feels when they read your posts, hear your podcast, watch your stories, or read your website copy.

Think of it like this: your brand voice is the energy you walk into a room with. Is it bold and fiery? Calm and nurturing? Witty and sarcastic? Grounded and wise? No voice is wrong — but your voice must be real.

This voice needs to reflect who you are at your core — and how you want to make others feel.

  • Are you here to inspire?
  • To educate?
  • To activate?
  • To comfort?
  • To challenge?

Your brand voice should carry your values and create emotional resonance. People remember how you made them feel far longer than they’ll remember your credentials. When your voice speaks their language, they stop scrolling. They pause. They listen.

And here’s the most powerful part: you already have this voice inside you. You use it with your closest friends, your clients, your community. You just haven’t named it yet. Until now.

Activity: Write a “Day in the Life” in your brand voice. Not your professional tone. Not your corporate script. Your voice. Describe your morning coffee. A funny moment with your partner. A breakthrough in your work. Let your natural tone rise to the surface. Because this — this energy — is what your audience is craving.

You don’t need to sound like anyone else. You just need to sound like you — turned all the way up
