Part 3: Music and Images

Music and images are powerful tools to enhance a digital story. First, let’s take a look at how music can be used to enhance digital storytelling.

Music and Digital Storytelling

Music is a powerful tool in digital storytelling that can shape the emotional impact, pacing, and engagement level of a narrative. When used effectively, it enhances the overall storytelling experience and can be a key element in conveying the message and impact of a story.

Let’s look at the techniques and benefits of effectively incorporating music into digital storytelling.

  • Set the Mood and Atmosphere: Music can establish the emotional tone of a story. It can convey excitement, sadness, tension, or joy. By selecting the right musical score or soundtrack, storytellers can guide the audience's emotional experience. For example, imagine a high-stakes heist scene in a movie. As the thieves attempt to steal a valuable artifact, the fast-paced and suspenseful soundtrack featuring dramatic strings and percussion conveys the tension and excitement of the moment.
  • Enhance Engagement: Music can capture the audience's attention and engage them on an emotional level. It helps to create a deeper connection with the story and keeps viewers more engaged.
  • Build a Narrative: In digital storytelling, music can act as a narrative device. It can signal transitions, foreshadow events, or emphasize key moments in the story. For example, a change in the music can indicate a shift in the storyline.
  • Character Development: Different characters in a story can be associated with specific musical themes or motifs. This can help the audience relate to and better understand the characters' emotions and development.
  • Create Rhythm and Pace: The tempo and rhythm of music can influence the pacing of a story. It can speed up or slow down the narrative, matching the tempo of the visuals or text and build tension or relaxation as needed.
  • Cultural and Contextual Significance: The choice of music can convey cultural and contextual information. For example, using traditional music from a specific region can enhance the sense of place and time in a story.
  • Memorability: Music can make a story more memorable. When a particular piece of music becomes associated with a story, it can stick in the audience's memory, making the story more impactful and easier to recall.
  • Accessibility and Inclusivity: Music can also be used to enhance the accessibility of digital content. For example, audio descriptions and captions can be paired with music to make stories more inclusive for people with visual or hearing impairments.

Let’s look at an example of how music has been used effectively in digital storytelling.

In 2022, in celebration of the International Day of People with Disabilities, Apple featured the song I Am the Greatest by the Australian group Spinifex Gum. This heartfelt advertisement showcased a diverse group of individuals with disabilities, highlighting the ways in which Apple's products, such as voice control, Assistive Touch, and Magnifier, empower and assist them.

The music in the ad is inspiring and helps illustrate the essence of inclusivity while fostering confidence and celebrating perseverance.

Images in Digital Storytelling

Images have the ability to infuse personality into narratives and propel storytelling. Research, as cited by the International Forum for Visual Practitioners (n.d.), reveals that the human brain processes images nearly 60,000 times more quickly than text content. Consequently, the incorporation of images, GIFs, videos, and other visual elements within digital storytelling has the potential to significantly reshape both the narrative and the audience's overall experience.

Therefore, when you need to convey a concept or idea quickly, it's more effective to show rather than tell.

Here are some ways to effectively use visuals to enhance digital storytelling:

  • Evoke Emotions: Use images that convey emotions and moods that text alone might struggle to express. The right visuals can instantly connect with the audience on an emotional level.
  • Create Engagement: Visually appealing content grabs the audience's attention and keeps them engaged. It helps in reducing bounce rates and increasing the time spent on a web page or in a digital story.
  • Enhance Memorability: Choose images that make the content more memorable. People tend to remember information better when it's associated with relevant visuals.
  • Aid Storytelling: Use images that provide context, setting, and character visuals that complement the narrative.
  • Provide Clarification: Incorporating relevant and high-quality visuals into digital storytelling can greatly enhance the overall impact and effectiveness of the narrative, making it more engaging, memorable, and emotionally resonant.

Another reason why visuals are more effective than text in digital storytelling is that they are more shareable. Social media platforms like Instagram and Pinterest are built around visual content, and visuals are much more likely to be shared and go viral than text-based content. This makes visual storytelling a valuable tool for businesses looking to increase their reach and engagement in digital storytelling.

Here is an example of effective visual storytelling:

In 2018, Patagonia, the American outdoor retailer, launched the Blue Heart Campaign to raise awareness and halt the construction of over 3,000 proposed dams across the Balkan region. Using imagery, videos, and creating a documentary titled DamNation, Patagonia was able to bring awareness to the negative environmental consequences that dams have such as species extinction and climate change. The campaign met with success through its social media campaign and Patagonia was able to halt the construction of the dams.
