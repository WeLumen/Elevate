Part 2: Digital Storytelling as a Tool

In the following section, we will move from the theoretical to the concrete. We will begin by looking at the purpose or intention of your story taking into account the company’s basic goals and message. Following that, we will look at some very general contours that those stories might take in terms of challenge, choice, and outcome.

Thus far, we’ve discovered the important role stories have played since the beginning of time. The stories and characters may have evolved, but the impact of stories has not. The stories we tell can go a long way and can bring the same impact to marketing campaigns. Let’s consider that stories can serve a goal, just as a marketing campaign does. Storytelling can also drive alignment within teams and can help them work more effectively. If your goal is to advocate and drive engagement in your marketing campaigns, storytelling is key.

When creating your story keep these notes in mind:

  • What are your organization or company's goals?
  • What messages do you want the story to send to help the goal move forward?
  • Once the goal has been decided, thinking of your audience is important and this is where characters come into play.

Characters can play a key role. They are the people who make your goals possible. Everyone in your story is someone you can talk to or about in your storytelling. They can even be the hero or protagonist in your story. This can be anyone from an employee, a client, or a stakeholder. The list goes on!
