Part 4: Ethics and Conclusion

Storytelling is integral to how we interact with the world, so we must be careful with how we use it.

Below, we will go over ways in which we can make sure we use storytelling ethically.

Use materials legally

Make sure you use the materials in your stories responsibly by researching the legal implications of music and images made by someone else.

Consider the industry

Each industry has different ethical standards to consider when telling a story. The important thing is that you put people first and make sure to tell others’ stories how they want them told.

Be honest and truthful

If you use your story to sell a product or a service, do not lie or exaggerate to make a point or get more sales. Customers trust organizations and businesses that tell real stories about their products, and by lying and exaggerating, you can lose that trust.

Tell people's stories how they want them told

If you use another person's story, make sure to meet their wishes about how they want their story to be used. Another important aspect of telling another's story is that you need their consent before sharing it. Ensure you inform them of your intended use of their story and that they agree (Voice of Witness, n.d.).

Remember: some stories do not need to be told. Sometimes, a straight presentation of facts can be your best tool for convincing others.

Understanding the significance of storytelling in the digital age can empower you to become a more effective communicator.

Storytelling is not just about sharing tales; it's a powerful tool for expressing your thoughts and experiences in a way that resonates with others. It enables you to craft narratives that vividly capture the complexities of the world around you, making your messages more engaging and relatable.

Whether you're a student, professional, or simply someone sharing stories with friends, mastering the art of storytelling is an invaluable skill. It's a chance to infuse creativity into your conversations and presentations, helping you connect with your audience on a deeper level. So, as you embark on this storytelling journey, remember to have fun with it. Let your imagination soar and be sure to use storytelling wisely with respect for the impact it can have. By doing so, you'll not only enhance your communication skills but also leave a positive mark on the digital landscape.
